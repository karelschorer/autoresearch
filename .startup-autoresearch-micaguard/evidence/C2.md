### Ongoing MiCA compliance operations wedge (CURRENT)

**Recurring CASP obligations under MiCA (validated):**

1. **Record-keeping (Article 68(9)):** All crypto-asset services, activities, orders, and transactions must be recorded and maintained for 5-7 years. RTS 2025/1140 specifies exact record formats. This is CONTINUOUS.

2. **Complaints handling (Article 71):** Standardized complaint handling procedures, client-facing templates, record-keeping of all complaints and measures taken. RTS 2025/294 specifies templates and procedures. This is ONGOING.

3. **Periodic reporting to NCAs:** CASPs must report on financial condition, exposure, and safeguarding. Portfolio management firms must provide quarterly client statements (at minimum every 3 months). Incident reporting required for security/operational events.

4. **Annual compliance audits:** Firms must review and adjust compliance policies annually to align with MiCA.

5. **Governance reporting:** Any changes in management body composition must be reported to NCA.

6. **Business continuity (Article 68(7) + DORA):** Ongoing ICT resilience requirements including testing and incident management. RTS 2025/299 specifies continuity requirements.

7. **Client communication standards (Articles 70-71):** All communications must meet fairness, clarity, and transparency standards — this includes ONGOING marketing and client materials (overlaps with marketing compliance but broader).

### Why the operations wedge works for SaaS
- Multiple recurring obligations (not one-time)
- Quarterly reporting, continuous record-keeping, ongoing complaint tracking
- Level 2 measures (RTS/ITS) are STILL being published — complexity growing
- Mid-market CASPs (1-3 compliance staff) need tooling to manage this without hiring more staff
- A "MiCA compliance dashboard" that automates reporting, tracks deadlines, manages complaints, and monitors regulatory changes is a genuine recurring-value product

### Why 7 and not higher
- "Compliance operations platform" is broader than a narrow wedge — risk of trying to do too much
- Need to validate that mid-market CASPs actually struggle with these recurring tasks (vs. using spreadsheets and managing fine)
- The wedge needs to be narrowed to ONE of these recurring tasks for launch (e.g., NCA periodic reporting automation or complaints handling)
- No PoC exists for any of these workflows
