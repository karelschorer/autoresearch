# A1: MiCA compliance is a documented burden
**Score: 9/10** (updated Cycle 10, UP from 8)

## Evidence

### Quantified pain
- **40%+ of exchanges** reported difficulty meeting MiCA reporting requirements due to high cost
- **75% of EU crypto exchanges** revamped internal compliance teams in 2025 (hiring regulatory experts, auditors)
- **35% of blockchain startups** estimate compliance costs >$500K/year
- **25% of global crypto firms** plan to withdraw from EU due to MiCA costs
- **18%+ of existing platforms** shut down or exited EU by 2025
- Licensing processing: **9-12 months** average, 150+ pending approvals backlog
- **25% of 2024 applicants** faced delays/rejections over documentation issues

### Disproportionate impact on mid-market
- "The compliance burden falls disproportionately on smaller operators" — multiple sources
- "Lean or loosely structured exchanges may struggle" — MiCA raises operational cost of being a CASP
- Personal liability: management bodies personally accountable for MiCA compliance
- Dual licensing (MiCA + PSD2) may double costs for some firms
- Market consolidation: ~3,100 VASPs → 150-180 CASPs (95% elimination)

### Ongoing pain (not one-time)
- EUR 5-15K/month ongoing compliance costs
- Annual compliance audits mandatory
- Quarterly NCA reporting
- Continuous record-keeping (5-7 years retention)
- ESMA publishing new standards continuously
- CARF/DAC8, DORA, AMLA add additional layers

### Why 9 and not 10
- The pain is clearly documented, quantified, and existential for the ICP
- 40%+ reporting difficulty + 75% team revamps + personal liability = strong signal
- Not a 10 because: (a) the pain could be partially addressed by hiring more staff rather than buying SaaS, (b) some firms may choose to exit EU rather than comply, reducing TAM, (c) need customer interviews to confirm SaaS willingness vs. "we'll hire another compliance officer"
