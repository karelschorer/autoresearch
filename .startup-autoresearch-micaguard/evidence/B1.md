# B1: Existing alternatives are inadequate
**Score: 6/10** (updated Cycle 8, UP from 5)

## Evidence

### The competitive landscape by layer (updated)

| Layer | Players | What They Do | What They DON'T Do |
|-------|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| **KYC/Onboarding** | Sumsub, Muinmos | Identity verification, risk profiling | No MiCA-specific ongoing compliance |
| **Transaction monitoring** | Chainalysis, Elliptic, TRM Labs | Blockchain analytics, AML | No MiCA reporting, complaints, governance |
| **Travel Rule** | Notabene | FATF data exchange | Single regulation only |
| **Marketing compliance** | Sedric AI | Website/social scanning | No disclosure generation, no NCA reporting |
| **Tax reporting** | Cryptio, Bitwave | CARF/DAC8, accounting | No MiCA operational compliance |
| **MiCA operations** | ??? | NCA reporting, complaints, change tracking | **NO TOOL EXISTS** |

### The validated gap: integrated MiCA operational compliance
- CASPs face EUR 5-15K/month in ongoing compliance costs
- Multiple regulations simultaneously: MiCA + DORA + CARF + AML Package
- Each has different reporting templates, deadlines, NCA requirements
- NCA fragmentation: different expectations across jurisdictions (NL vs. DE vs. FR vs. IE)
- No single tool integrates these recurring operations
- Current approach: spreadsheets + law firm retainers + manual tracking
- "Embedded compliance" is the stated need — controls operating within transaction flows, not after

### Key challenge from stress test
- Many of these compliance operations are generic (complaints handling, record-keeping, reporting) — could be served by horizontal compliance platforms (ComplyAdvantage, Alloy, etc.)
- The MiCA-specific differentiation is in: (a) understanding which ESMA technical standards apply to which CASP type, (b) tracking ESMA/NCA guidance changes, (c) mapping requirements to the firm's specific license and services
- The gap is in the REGULATORY INTELLIGENCE layer, not the operational workflow layer

### Why recovered to 6
B1 was at 5 because Sedric occupied the marketing wedge. But the broader picture reveals a genuine gap in integrated MiCA operational compliance. No tool combines NCA reporting + complaints handling + regulatory change tracking + multi-regulation coordination (MiCA/DORA/CARF/AML). The gap is real for mid-market CASPs who can't afford to assemble 5 different tools + law firm retainers. Not a 7 because horizontal compliance platforms could expand to cover this space.
