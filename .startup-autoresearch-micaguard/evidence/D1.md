# D1: Market reachable
**Score: 7/10** (updated Cycle 1, up from 5)

## Evidence

### MiCA-licensed entity count (hard data)
- **102 authorized CASPs** as of December 2025 per ESMA register (Zampa Partners analysis)
- **150-180 fully licensed CASPs** expected by mid-2026 as grandfathering deadlines expire
- 17 authorized in April 2025 → 40 by July 2025 → 60 by Sept 2025 → 102 by Dec 2025 → growing rapidly
- July 1, 2026 is the ABSOLUTE final deadline — no further extensions

### Pre-MiCA market size
- ~3,100 VASPs (Virtual Asset Service Providers) operated in EU before MiCA
- ~75% (~2,500) will lose registration status — massive consolidation
- Fewer than 500 unregulated VASPs expected to remain active by end 2026

### Geographic concentration
- Netherlands, Germany, Malta, France, Ireland lead in license issuances
- 14 major exchanges fully authorized (Binance FR, Kraken IE, Coinbase IE, Bitstamp LU, OKX MT)
- Strong concentration in Jeroen's home market (NL) and nearby jurisdictions

### Reachability
- Crypto industry is highly networked — conferences (EBC, Token2049, Consensus), Telegram/Discord communities, LinkedIn
- Jeroen's Kraken network provides direct access to licensed CASP decision-makers
- Dutch Blockchain Coalition, Holland FinTech are additional channels
- ESMA register is public — all licensed entities are identifiable by name

### Why 7 and not higher
- 150-180 entities clears the >=100 threshold, but the beachhead (Dutch firms, ~40) is still small
- Not all 150-180 CASPs need the specific tooling MiCAGuard offers (large exchanges have in-house compliance teams)
- The "sweet spot" ICP (10-100 employees, can't afford Big Four) may be 60-100 of the 150-180
- Need to validate how many are mid-market vs. large enterprise vs. tiny one-person operations
- International expansion (beyond NL) requires multi-language support and jurisdiction-specific knowledge
