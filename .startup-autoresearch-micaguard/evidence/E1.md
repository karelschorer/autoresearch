# E1: Team advantage
**Score: 8/10** (updated Cycle 7, DOWN from 9 after stress test)

## Evidence

### Strengths (original thesis)
- Jeroen sold Masterpiece to Kraken — knows how crypto firms operate from the inside
- Karel builds AI/LLM products end-to-end
- Both can ship independently — rare duo of two builders
- Jeroen's crypto network provides warm intros to CASP decision-makers

### Stress test: adversarial challenges

**1. Jeroen's Kraken connection — narrower than assumed**
- Kraken got its MiCA license from Central Bank of Ireland, operates all 30 EEA countries from Ireland entity
- Kraken acquired Dutch broker BCM in 2024 and partnered with bunq (Dutch neobank)
- Jeroen sold a company TO Kraken — he knows Kraken's team, not necessarily the broader EU CASP landscape
- His network is valuable for 5-10 warm intros, not 50-100. The remaining CASPs (Bitvavo, Coinbase, MoonPay, Bybit, etc.) require standard sales outreach

**2. "AI builder" is not a differentiator for compliance SaaS**
- Building an LLM-based compliance tool is standard engineering in 2026
- Harvey, Sedric, Casetext, and dozens of legal AI startups have already built comparable pipelines
- Karel's eval harness experience is relevant but not proprietary — thousands of ML engineers build eval pipelines
- The differentiation must come from domain specificity (MiCA expertise), not from generic AI capability

**3. Neither founder is a compliance expert**
- Neither Karel nor Jeroen has a regulatory affairs / compliance background
- Crypto firms buying compliance tools need to trust the vendor's regulatory knowledge
- Competing against Sedric (500+ FI customers, RegTech100) and Big Four advisors (decades of regulatory credibility)
- Will need to hire or partner with a crypto compliance expert to build credibility

**4. Kraken-specific knowledge is not MiCA-wide knowledge**
- Jeroen knows how ONE crypto firm operates (exchange/broker)
- MiCA covers diverse CASP types: exchanges, custodians, stablecoin issuers, token projects, portfolio managers
- Knowledge of Kraken's operations doesn't transfer to stablecoin compliance or token issuance workflows

### What survives the stress test
- **Speed:** Two builders who can ship a working product in weeks. This is real and valuable.
- **Network access:** 5-10 warm intros to crypto firm decision-makers is enough for first design partners
- **Insider perspective:** Jeroen understands crypto firm culture (move fast, cost-conscious, distrust of trad-fi compliance vendors). This shapes better product decisions than a compliance expert building for the first time.
- **Bootstrap speed:** No fundraising delay. Can reach first revenue before a VC-funded competitor completes a seed round.

### Why dropped to 8
The 9/10 score assumed Jeroen's network covers the full CASP landscape. In reality, it covers Kraken's ecosystem (~5-10 intros). The AI capability is table-stakes, not a moat. Neither founder has compliance domain expertise. The surviving advantage is speed + insider perspective + bootstrap execution — genuine but not as strong as initially scored.
