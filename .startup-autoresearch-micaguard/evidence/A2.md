# A2: The compliance burden is growing
**Score: 9/10** (updated Cycle 11, UP from 8)

## Evidence

### Regulatory expansion is structural, not cyclical

**MiCA Level 2/3 measures still rolling out (2026):**
- 30+ ESMA Technical Standards and Guidelines published, more coming
- March 2026: New guidelines on security protocols, crypto classification, market abuse, financial instruments
- June 2026: Delegated Act on definitions (Art. 139)
- RTS on record-keeping (2025/1140), complaints (2025/294), business continuity (2025/299) — all recently in force

**Additional regulations compounding through 2027:**
- CARF/DAC8 (Jan 2026): Tax reporting framework — first cross-border exchange 2027
- PSD2 interplay (Feb/March 2026): Dual licensing for EMT services
- AMLA (2026-2027): New AML/CFT authority with own technical standards
- AMLR/AMLD6 (July 2027): Full new AML regulation applies
- DORA (Jan 2025): Operational resilience requirements ongoing

**MiCA II on the horizon:**
- Commission report on MiCA application due June 2027, potentially with legislative proposal
- DeFi, staking, and lending expected to be covered in MiCA II
- This means the regulatory surface will EXPAND again in 2027-2028

### Enforcement escalation
- EUR 540M+ in fines issued since MiCA implementation
- 50+ license revocations
- France: EUR 62M single fine (largest to date)
- ESMA pushing for more centralized supervision

### Why 9 and not 10
- The growth trajectory is clear, structural, and multi-year
- Multiple regulations compound the burden every 6-12 months
- Not a 10 because: (a) regulatory growth could plateau post-2027 if MiCA II is delayed, (b) enforcement could remain uneven across NCAs, (c) the crypto industry could shrink if regulation becomes too burdensome (fewer firms = smaller TAM)
