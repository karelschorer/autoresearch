# Validation Criteria — MiCAGuard

## A. Problem Validation — Is the pain real and urgent?

### A1: MiCA compliance is a documented burden for crypto firms
**Question:** Is there documented evidence that compliance teams at EU crypto firms spend significant time/money dealing with MiCA requirements, OR that failure to comply causes material harm (license revocation, fines)?
**Pass condition:** Multiple sources confirm MiCA compliance is a significant, recurring burden for crypto firms with 10-100 employees — dedicated compliance hires, external advisory spend, license application complexity.
**Fail condition:** MiCA compliance is straightforward, well-handled by existing processes, or outsourced cheaply.
**Research method:** Crypto industry reports, MiCA compliance survey data, job posting analysis, regulatory consultancy pricing, crypto firm interviews.

### A2: The compliance burden is growing
**Question:** Is there concrete evidence that MiCA-related requirements are expanding (ESMA technical standards, national regulator guidelines, enforcement actions) over the next 2-3 years?
**Pass condition:** ESMA is actively publishing new technical standards, national regulators are issuing MiCA-specific guidance, enforcement actions are beginning, additional regulations (TFR, DORA) compound the burden.
**Fail condition:** MiCA is a one-time implementation; once compliant, the ongoing burden is minimal.
**Research method:** ESMA publication schedule, national regulator announcements, enforcement action tracking, regulatory pipeline analysis.

## B. Existing Alternatives — Is the current solution bad enough?

### B1: Existing crypto compliance tools don't cover MiCA holistically
**Question:** Are the existing compliance solutions (Chainalysis, Notabene, Sumsub, Big Four advisory) clearly inadequate for MiCA-specific compliance — particularly marketing rules, disclosure generation, and ongoing regulatory monitoring?
**Pass condition:** Existing tools focus on AML/KYC/transaction monitoring, not MiCA-specific requirements (marketing rules, white paper disclosures, prudential reporting). Big Four advisory is too expensive for mid-market crypto firms.
**Fail condition:** Existing tools already cover MiCA comprehensively, or new MiCA-specific tools have launched and gained traction.
**Research method:** Competitor feature analysis, product documentation, G2/Capterra reviews, crypto compliance community discussions.

### B2: There is a specific gap in MiCA compliance tooling
**Question:** Is there a clearly identifiable workflow gap that no tool addresses for MiCA compliance — specifically in marketing compliance, disclosure generation, or regulatory change monitoring?
**Pass condition:** No tool automates MiCA marketing compliance checking, crypto-asset white paper generation, or MiCA-specific regulatory change tracking. Compliance teams do this manually.
**Fail condition:** Existing tools or generic LLMs adequately cover these workflows.
**Research method:** Competitor feature deep-dive, regulatory workflow analysis, compliance officer pain points.

## C. Solution-Market Fit — Can this specific solution win?

### C1: AI can achieve required accuracy for MiCA compliance checking
**Question:** Can an LLM pipeline cross-referencing MiCA regulation text, ESMA technical standards, and national guidance achieve sufficient accuracy for compliance checking — particularly for marketing material review and disclosure generation?
**Pass condition:** MiCA regulation is well-structured and finite; comparable legal AI tools (Harvey, Casetext) demonstrate the pattern works; the marketing rules are clear enough for deterministic + LLM approach.
**Fail condition:** MiCA interpretation requires deep legal judgment that LLMs cannot reliably provide; compliance officers would not trust AI-generated assessments.
**Research method:** Legal AI benchmarks, MiCA regulation structure analysis, comparable tool accuracy data.

### C2: The wedge delivers standalone value at sufficient frequency
**Question:** Does the marketing compliance checker (or alternative wedge) deliver value frequently enough to justify a monthly SaaS subscription?
**Pass condition:** Crypto firms produce marketing content regularly (weekly/monthly); the compliance check is needed for every piece of content; the wedge solves a high-frequency, recurring task.
**Fail condition:** Marketing compliance is a quarterly task; the wedge is too narrow for recurring subscription value.
**Research method:** Crypto marketing frequency analysis, compliance workflow mapping, content volume data.

## D. Market & Economics — Is the market real and reachable?

### D1: There are >=100 identifiable MiCA-licensed entities reachable through <=2 channels
**Question:** How many MiCA-licensed entities (CASPs, stablecoin issuers, etc.) exist or are expected in the EU by end 2026, and can >=100 be reached through Jeroen's network + crypto community channels?
**Pass condition:** >200 MiCA-licensed entities across EU by end 2026, concentrated in NL/DE/FR, reachable through crypto industry networks and conferences.
**Fail condition:** <100 total MiCA-licensed entities in EU; market too small for viable SaaS.
**Research method:** EBA/ESMA license registers, national regulator data (DNB, BaFin, AMF), industry estimates.

### D2: Unit economics work — value >= 3x price
**Question:** Does the value MiCAGuard delivers (time saved, advisory cost avoided, compliance risk reduced) justify at least 3x the proposed price?
**Pass condition:** A crypto firm spending EUR 50-200K/year on compliance advisory could save 30-50% with MiCAGuard; at EUR 299-999/mo the value-to-price ratio is 3-10x.
**Fail condition:** Compliance advisory costs are lower than assumed; crypto firms are too cost-sensitive; willingness-to-pay doesn't justify the subscription.
**Research method:** Compliance cost benchmarks, advisory pricing data, crypto firm budget analysis.

## E. Founder & Timing — Can this team win now?

### E1: Karel + Jeroen have a defensible advantage for crypto compliance AI
**Question:** Does Jeroen's Kraken exit + crypto network combined with Karel's AI/LLM product building create a defensible advantage over well-funded competitors entering this space?
**Pass condition:** Jeroen's network provides warm intros to decision-makers at MiCA-regulated firms; Karel can ship a working product in weeks; together they can reach first paying customers before a funded competitor builds the same tool.
**Fail condition:** Jeroen's network is exhaustible (5-10 intros); Big Four or established RegTech firms launch MiCA tools with bigger distribution; the technical product is commoditized by generic LLM tools.
**Research method:** Network analysis, competitive moat assessment, speed-to-market evaluation.

### E2: MiCA creates a timing catalyst for NOW
**Question:** Does MiCA's full effectiveness (Jan 2026) plus ongoing ESMA technical standard publication create a window of opportunity in the next 12-24 months?
**Pass condition:** MiCA compliance demand is peaking now; firms that delayed are scrambling; ESMA technical standards create ongoing compliance work beyond initial implementation.
**Fail condition:** Most firms completed MiCA compliance before Jan 2026; the wave has passed; ongoing requirements are minimal.
**Research method:** MiCA implementation timeline analysis, ESMA publication schedule, enforcement action data.
