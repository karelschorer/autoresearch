# MiCAGuard — AI-Powered MiCA Compliance for EU Crypto Firms

## Problem Statement
MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation) became fully effective January 2026, requiring all EU crypto firms to comply with extensive requirements: white paper disclosures, reserve asset management, transaction monitoring, marketing rules, and ongoing reporting. Compliance teams at crypto exchanges, wallet providers, and token issuers are drowning in manual regulatory interpretation. Most are small teams (1-3 people) at firms that grew in a largely unregulated environment. The penalty for non-compliance: loss of EU operating license.

## Target Customer (ICP)
**Beachhead:** Mid-market MiCA-licensed CASPs (10-100 employees, 1-3 person compliance team) in NL, DE, and IE. As of Dec 2025, 102 CASPs authorized on ESMA register; expected 150-180 by mid-2026 as July 1, 2026 grandfathering deadline expires. Estimated 60-100 are mid-market (too small for Big Four, too complex for manual compliance). Large exchanges (Binance, Kraken, Coinbase) have in-house teams; micro-CASPs (<5 employees) can't afford SaaS.
**Expand-to:** Full EU CASP population (150-180 licensed entities), plus the ~500 VASPs that survive MiCA consolidation (from ~3,100 pre-MiCA). Secondary: non-EU crypto firms seeking EU market access via MiCA passporting.

## Proposed Solution & Wedge
AI compliance assistant that ingests MiCA regulation text, ESMA technical standards, and national regulator guidance, then helps compliance teams: (1) generate required disclosures (white papers, risk assessments), (2) monitor marketing materials for MiCA compliance, (3) track regulatory changes and assess impact. Wedge: automated MiCA marketing compliance checker — scan website/social media for non-compliant crypto promotional claims.
