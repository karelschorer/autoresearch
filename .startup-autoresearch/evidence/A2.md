# A2: The problem is growing due to regulatory expansion

**Verdict: YES**

## Evidence

- The ECGT directive (EU 2024/825) enters enforcement on 27 September 2026, banning generic environmental claims without specific substantiation — a new verification requirement for any FMCG company using sustainability messaging. Fines up to 4% of annual EU turnover.
- NVWA fined 25 out of 30 collagen supplement websites (Aug 2025) and opened investigations into Nestle and Unilever (June 2025) for prohibited health claims — active and escalating enforcement.
- EFSA is advancing ingredient-level restrictions (monacolins ban, green tea EGCG expected restrictions, berberine/fennel decisions in 2026) that will force portfolio-wide claim audits.
- PPWR packaging compliance (12 August 2026) requires substantiated recyclability claims per SKU; McKinsey estimated 2-5% per-SKU cost increase.
- ~2,078 on-hold botanical claims are approaching EU-level resolution; when resolved, companies will need full portfolio audits.
- DGCCRF (France) increased maximum penalties to €600,000 or 10% of global annual turnover (DDADUE Law, April 2024).
