# A2: The problem is growing due to regulatory expansion

**Score: 7/10** (dropped from 9 after Cycle 13 stress test)

## Evidence — expansion is real but thesis is overconfident

### What's genuinely expanding (strong evidence)
- ECGT enforcement September 27, 2026 — hard deadline, already transposed
- NVWA active enforcement in 2025 (25/30 collagen sites fined, Nestle/Unilever investigated)
- ACM enforcement letters to food companies April 2025 (fines up to €900K)
- PPWR packaging compliance August 12, 2026

### Stress test findings — weaknesses in the thesis

**Simplification Omnibus could REDUCE complexity:**
- The Food and Feed Safety Simplification Omnibus (December 2025) proposes amending 10 existing regulations
- If it streamlines the NHCR claims authorization process, it could reduce the complexity ClaimPilot monetizes
- A simpler, faster claims process = less need for ongoing compliance tooling

**ECGT enforcement will be patchy:**
- Enforcement lies entirely with national competent authorities
- Commission FAQ (early 2026) still leaves "open questions" per Bird & Bird analysis
- GDPR early enforcement was notoriously patchy across Member States — ECGT carries same risk
- Germany and France enforce aggressively; Southern/Eastern EU historically lag
- Supplement brands can jurisdiction-shop or wait out enforcement cycles

**Botanical claims are perpetually "imminent":**
- On hold since 2012 — now 14 years with no resolution
- CJEU April 2025 ruling (C-386/23) tightened restrictions but Commission evaluation process remains suspended
- Industry experts confirm "remains in limbo" as of early 2026
- Ten-plus years of "imminent resolution" is a warning, not a tailwind

**GCD withdrawal signals political flexibility:**
- Green Claims Directive withdrawn June 2025 under SME burden pressure
- Shows the EU WILL sacrifice regulatory instruments under political pressure
- ECGT fills the gap but the precedent reduces certainty about future regulation

### Why 7/10
The regulatory expansion is real — ECGT and NVWA enforcement are not theoretical. But the thesis was overconfident at 9/10. The Simplification Omnibus is a genuine risk to the "increasing complexity" narrative. ECGT enforcement patchiness means the urgency varies by country. The botanical claims resolution has been "imminent" for 14 years. The direction is more regulation, but the path is bumpier and less certain than 9/10 implied.
