# A2: The problem is growing due to regulatory expansion

**Score: 8/10** (recovered from 7 in Cycle 15 — reframed to NL enforcement)

## Reframed narrative: "Dutch enforcement is happening NOW"

### NL enforcement is genuinely aggressive
- **NVWA** issues 6,600+ enforcement measures/year (18/day)
- NVWA fined 25/30 collagen supplement websites (Aug 2025, 83% violation rate)
- NVWA investigated Nestle and Unilever for false health claims (June 2025)
- NVWA fines: up to €1.03M per violation, theoretical cap €5.15M per inspection
- **ACM** sent food-sector warning letters April 2025, fines up to €900K
- **Dual-regulator posture** (NVWA health claims + ACM sustainability claims) is unusual in EU

### ECGT is law (not proposed)
- Enforcement starts September 27, 2026 — hard deadline, no delay
- NL is well-positioned for early enforcement (KLM greenwashing ruling, existing ACM activity)
- Bans generic claims ("natural," "sustainable," "eco-friendly") without substantiation

### Existing framework is ALREADY complex enough
- Regulation 1924/2006: 4,637 evaluated claims, 2,078 on hold
- Unsettled nutrient profiles after 20 years
- EFSA submission process "complex, costly, and time-consuming"
- Academics: "the regulation is not working"

### Stress test weaknesses (acknowledged)
- Simplification Omnibus (Dec 2025) could reduce future complexity
- ECGT enforcement will be patchy across EU Member States
- Botanical claims perpetually "imminent" (14 years, no resolution)
- GCD withdrawal shows political flexibility

### Why 8/10
The NL-specific enforcement narrative is strong and grounded in current actions, not predictions. The existing regulatory framework is already complex enough to justify ClaimPilot without any new laws. The 8/10 ceiling acknowledges the Simplification Omnibus risk and ECGT enforcement patchiness outside NL. The message is "don't get fined NOW in NL" — concrete, falsifiable, and anchored in real enforcement actions.
