# A2: The problem is growing due to regulatory expansion

**Score: 9/10** (improved from 8 in Cycle 25 — Novel Nutriology ruling + marketplace gating)

## Reframed narrative: "Dutch enforcement is happening NOW"

### NL enforcement is genuinely aggressive
- **NVWA** issues 6,600+ enforcement measures/year (18/day)
- NVWA fined 25/30 collagen supplement websites (Aug 2025, 83% violation rate)
- NVWA investigated Nestle and Unilever for false health claims (June 2025)
- NVWA fines: up to €1.03M per violation, theoretical cap €5.15M per inspection
- **ACM** sent food-sector warning letters April 2025, fines up to €900K
- **Dual-regulator posture** (NVWA health claims + ACM sustainability claims) is unusual in EU

### ECGT is law (not proposed)
- Enforcement starts September 27, 2026 — hard deadline, no delay
- NL is well-positioned for early enforcement (KLM greenwashing ruling, existing ACM activity)
- Bans generic claims ("natural," "sustainable," "eco-friendly") without substantiation

### Existing framework is ALREADY complex enough
- Regulation 1924/2006: 4,637 evaluated claims, 2,078 on hold
- Unsettled nutrient profiles after 20 years
- EFSA submission process "complex, costly, and time-consuming"
- Academics: "the regulation is not working"

### Stress test weaknesses (acknowledged)
- Simplification Omnibus (Dec 2025) could reduce future complexity
- ECGT enforcement will be patchy across EU Member States
- Botanical claims perpetually "imminent" (14 years, no resolution)
- GCD withdrawal shows political flexibility

### The problem is actively getting WORSE (Cycle 25 — new evidence)
- **CJEU Novel Nutriology ruling (April 2025):** Killed the botanical on-hold loophole. ~2,078 on-hold claims now default to prohibited. Every botanical supplement brand must re-evaluate ALL their health claims. This is the single biggest compliance disruption since EC 1924/2006 took effect.
- **Marketplace gating tightening:** Amazon EU now requires third-party verification from NSF/UL/Eurofins. Bol.com delists non-compliant supplements. This ADDS a compliance burden on top of NVWA enforcement.
- **NVWA broadening enforcement scope:** Thematic sweeps now include influencers (56% non-compliant), collagen (83%), protein claims. Not just labels — social media, websites, ads.
- **Influencer liability emerging:** NVWA explicitly warned influencers "next time we will enforce" (2025). This extends the compliance surface from product labels to all marketing channels.

### Why 9/10 (not higher)
The problem is now demonstrably growing on THREE fronts: (1) CJEU ruling invalidating botanical claims, (2) marketplace compliance requirements tightening, (3) NVWA broadening enforcement to influencers and online channels. The existing EC 1924/2006 framework was already complex (4,637 evaluated claims). The 9/10 ceiling: the Simplification Omnibus (Dec 2025) could reduce future complexity, and CJEU may trigger reform that eventually simplifies the landscape. But in the near term (2025-2027), the problem is unambiguously getting worse.
