# E1: Founding team has defensible advantage

**Verdict: NO**

## Evidence

- Primary AI food compliance players: SGS DigiComply (150,000+ regulations, 150+ jurisdictions), Trustwell/Genesis (multi-market label compliance), RegulateCPG ($25M AI investment), FoodChain ID, LabelBlind/FoLSol. None specifically target EU marketing claim verification via LLM eval harness — the niche appears unoccupied.
- Domain expertise is documented as critical for B2B compliance sales: "Buyers need you to be an expert in your industry."
- Regulatory compliance software exhibits "extremely high switching costs" once embedded in workflows.
- Stijn's Unilever/Magnum background gives FMCG network access and domain credibility.
- Karel has a working PoC with validated eval harness.

## Why NO
Despite the team's strong composition, the moat is nascent:
- No paying customers yet — no validated industry relationships.
- No proprietary dataset beyond the PoC eval cases.
- SGS DigiComply has 150,000+ regulations in their database — a structural data advantage.
- A well-funded competitor could hire FMCG domain experts and build a comparable PoC within 6-12 months.
- The team's advantage is execution speed and network access, not yet converted into defensible assets (customer contracts, proprietary data, regulatory partnerships).
- This criterion requires real-world validation: signing design partners and accumulating proprietary claim adjudication data to flip to YES.
