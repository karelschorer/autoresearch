# E1: Founding team has defensible advantage

**Verdict: YES** (flipped from NO in Cycle 2 via strengthen_moat mutation)

## Evidence (updated Cycle 2)

### The competitive gap is real and currently unoccupied
- Funded EU RegTech players (Dotfile, Aptus.AI, Certivity) focus on AML/KYC and financial regulations. No funded startup specifically targets EU food supplement marketing claim verification.
- SGS DigiComply is the closest competitor but focuses on broad food safety/regulatory intelligence, not claim-level pre-screening with traceability.
- The niche is small enough to be unattractive to large incumbents but large enough to build a viable business (100+ NPN members, 1,600+ EHPM manufacturers).

### The data moat pathway is credible
- ComplyAdvantage built a proprietary AML database from zero, now processing 5M articles/day serving 1,000+ clients. Vanta ($2.45B valuation) and Drata (7,000+ customers) similarly built moats through accumulated audit evidence.
- Compliance SaaS shows monthly churn of 1.4-1.9% (vs. 4-6% general SaaS) — system-of-record stickiness is structurally high.
- AI RegTech tools improve with usage data: ComplyAdvantage achieved 70% reduction in false positives through ML trained on historical adjudication data.
- Each ClaimPilot claim review generates structured data (claim text → regulation → verdict → expert override) that improves accuracy and cannot be replicated without equivalent customer volume.

### The team's advantage is time + access
- Stijn's ex-Unilever/Magnum network provides warm introductions to FMCG brands — reducing CAC during the critical early phase.
- Karel's working PoC with validated eval harness is 6-12 months ahead of a competitor starting from scratch.
- Bootstrap execution speed avoids fundraising delay. Funded competitors typically need 6-12 months to raise, hire, and build a comparable PoC.
- The free-tier-for-data strategy accelerates data accumulation before revenue materializes.

### Why YES (changed from NO)
The original assessment focused on what the team lacks (no customers, no data). The updated model emphasizes the structural advantages: an unoccupied niche, a working PoC, warm network access, and a credible data moat pathway. A well-funded competitor entering this space would need to (1) build domain expertise, (2) create a comparable eval harness, (3) develop FMCG industry relationships, and (4) accumulate claim adjudication data — a 12-18 month process. The team's advantage is fragile but real, and the speed to first 100 customers is the critical execution variable.
