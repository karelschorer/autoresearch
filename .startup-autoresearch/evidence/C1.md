# C1: Hybrid system can achieve sufficient accuracy on finite claim corpus

**Score: 8/10** (Cycle 16: hybrid architecture articulated, then stress-tested back to 8)

## Evidence (updated Cycle 16)

### Hybrid architecture advantage on finite corpus
- **Hybrid pipeline:** (1) deterministic match against authorized claims → (2) semantic embeddings for paraphrase detection → (3) LLM for condition-of-use logic and edge cases
- **TF-IDF + fuzzy matching** achieves 90-98% accuracy on small-database lookup tasks (information retrieval literature)
- **FAQ matching systems** targeting 90%+ hit rate on corpora of similar size (hundreds of entries) are a solved problem
- Architecture is sound: each layer handles progressively harder cases, limiting LLM exposure to edge cases only

### Stress test weaknesses (Cycle 16) — partially resolved by Novel Nutriology ruling
- **Corpus complexity reduced post-CJEU C-386/23 (Cycle 24):** The Novel Nutriology ruling (April 2025) killed the botanical on-hold loophole. Post-ruling, the matching landscape simplifies: ~262 authorized (YES), ~2,067 rejected (NO), botanical on-hold claims now default to prohibited (NO) unless properly filed under transitional provisions. The ambiguity zone shrinks significantly. This makes ClaimPilot's task EASIER, not harder.
- **Deterministic layer may catch <60%, not 80%+:** Real-world marketing claims are creative paraphrases. Copywriters optimize for appeal, not regulatory match. More volume pushed to embeddings/LLM layers. (Still valid concern.)
- **Condition-of-use complexity:** Conditions specify dose, population, food matrix, wording restrictions. Structured logic beyond simple lookup. (Still valid.)
- **False negative risk:** Wrongly flagging an authorized claim as non-compliant costs the brand a valid marketing claim. (Still valid.)

### Direct accuracy data for structured regulatory rule-matching
- **92.8% precision / 94.1% recall** achieved on structured regulatory compliance text classification using domain-specific LLM pipeline with 1.7M annotated documents (WJAETS 2025)
- **GoVisually** claims 90% accuracy improvement in food label compliance checking across 40+ regulatory frameworks including EFSA
- General LLMs on structured text classification (intent + rule lookup) routinely reach 88-95% when well-scoped

### ClaimPilot's task is structurally favorable for AI
- The EU Register is a **finite, structured corpus** — ideal for deterministic + RAG systems
- RAGBench and LegalBench-RAG confirm RAG accuracy is highest with small, structured, de-duplicated knowledge bases
- The task is: match claim text → authorized list → check conditions (dose thresholds). Closer to structured rule-matching than open-ended legal reasoning
- ClaimPilot's task is EASIER than pharma MLR (which requires open-ended clinical substantiation)

### AI-assisted model further de-risks accuracy concerns
- Veeva PromoMats: 50-75% cycle time reduction with AI pre-check + human approval
- EVERSANA: after engineering, 86% error reduction, >90% of routine tasks automated
- Human expert review catches remaining errors — final accuracy target >95% is achievable

### Why 8/10 (not higher)
The hybrid architecture is sound and the Novel Nutriology ruling simplifies the matching landscape (reducing botanical ambiguity). However: creative paraphrases still dominate real-world claims (pushing volume to embeddings/LLM layers), condition-of-use logic adds structured complexity, and no published PoC eval results exist. To reach 9+: publish PoC accuracy on real customer claim sets showing the hybrid pipeline achieves >90% pre-human-review accuracy.
