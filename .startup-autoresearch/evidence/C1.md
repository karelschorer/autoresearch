# C1: LLM + regulatory database can achieve sufficient accuracy

**Verdict: NO**

## Evidence

- GPT-5 achieves 84.6% on LegalBench (162 legal tasks) — the best current score, but below the 90% threshold for general legal text.
- Stanford 2024 study (peer-reviewed, Journal of Legal Analysis) finds LLMs hallucinate 58-88% on specific legal/regulatory queries without grounding.
- On LEXAM (340+ laws), GPT-5 scores only 70.20 average.
- RAG pipelines show 15% precision improvement in legal document analysis and are the industry-standard mitigation, but no published benchmark confirms >90% on EU health claim verification specifically.
- Commercial comparables (GoVisually: 99.9% on label keyword checks; Hawk:AI: ~90% alert accuracy; Veeva PromoMats: 80% auto-link rate) show near-90% is achievable on narrow tasks, but these are vendor-reported figures.
- The PoC eval harness exists and is validated against real regulatory cases (founder-provided evidence), but no specific accuracy figure has been published or independently verified.
- Veeva's own assessment notes regulatory professionals are not yet ready to remove human review from AI-generated verdicts.

## Why NO
The general LLM literature shows accuracy below the 90% threshold. The PoC may achieve >90% but this hasn't been independently validated. Regulatory professionals' trust barrier means even accurate AI output requires human oversight, partially negating time savings. This criterion requires real-world validation data from the PoC eval harness to flip to YES.
