# C1: LLM + regulatory database can achieve sufficient accuracy

**Score: 8/10** (improved from 7 in Cycle 7 — new accuracy benchmarks)

## Evidence (updated Cycle 7)

### Direct accuracy data for structured regulatory rule-matching
- **92.8% precision / 94.1% recall** achieved on structured regulatory compliance text classification using domain-specific LLM pipeline with 1.7M annotated documents (WJAETS 2025). This is the closest published benchmark to ClaimPilot's task.
- **GoVisually** claims 90% accuracy improvement in food label compliance checking across 40+ regulatory frameworks including EFSA.
- General LLMs on structured text classification (intent + rule lookup) routinely reach 88-95% when well-scoped.

### ClaimPilot's task is structurally favorable for AI
- The EU Register contains only ~270 approved claims with specific conditions of use — this is a **finite, structured corpus**, ideal for RAG systems.
- RAGBench and LegalBench-RAG confirm that RAG accuracy is highest with small, structured, de-duplicated knowledge bases — exactly what the EU Register is.
- The task is essentially: match claim text → authorized list → check conditions (dose thresholds). This is closer to structured rule-matching than open-ended legal reasoning.
- ClaimPilot's task is EASIER than pharma MLR (which requires open-ended clinical substantiation).

### AI-assisted model further de-risks accuracy concerns
- Veeva PromoMats: 50-75% cycle time reduction with AI pre-check + human approval
- FDA adopted AI-assisted review in 2025
- EVERSANA: initial AI accuracy 20-40%, but after engineering: 86% error reduction, >90% of routine tasks automated
- White & Case survey: trust requires human oversight — which ClaimPilot has built in

### Academic validation
- IEEE 2024 paper demonstrated automated nutritional claim verification against regulatory standards using SVM/Decision Tree on OCR-extracted text
- FoodVisor 2025: GPT-4o outperformed GPT-4V and Gemini on multilingual food label analysis

### Why 8/10 (not higher)
The 92.8% precision figure is from a general regulatory compliance task, not EU health claims specifically. The PoC eval harness exists but no published accuracy figure. GoVisually's 90% is vendor-stated, not independently audited. To reach 9+, the PoC needs to publish its eval harness results or run a third-party accuracy audit.
