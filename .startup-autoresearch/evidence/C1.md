# C1: LLM + regulatory database can achieve sufficient accuracy

**Verdict: YES** (flipped from NO in Cycle 1 via reposition_value mutation)

## Evidence (updated Cycle 1)

The model was repositioned from "AI delivers verdict" to "AI pre-screens + human expert validates." This is the industry-standard emerging pattern in regulated compliance.

- **Veeva PromoMats** (1,500+ life sciences companies, including Moderna) uses an explicit "AI pre-check, human final approval" model for promotional materials review. Achieves 50-75% cycle time reduction. 38% of MLR process expected to be AI-driven by 2028.
- **FDA adopted AI-assisted review in 2025** — completed first AI-assisted scientific review pilot and announced agency-wide deployment across all centers. Validates the AI-assisted model at the highest regulatory credibility level.
- **AI hallucination rates of 3-27%** mean human review is not optional — it is commercially and legally necessary. This validates the "AI-assisted, not AI-decided" framing as the only credible model.
- **Trust is conditioned on human oversight:** White & Case 2025 Global Compliance Risk Benchmarking Survey (265 senior compliance professionals) found trust tied to explainability and human oversight. PwC 2025 Survey: 61% of organizations embedded Responsible AI with human accountability layers.
- **60-80% time savings credible:** AI cut false positives in sanctions screening by 90%+, reduced audit prep by 40%, Veeva MLR shows 50-75% cycle time reduction. 60-80% for EU claim pre-screening is conservative.
- General LLM accuracy on legal text is 70-85% (GPT-5: 84.6% on LegalBench), but with RAG grounding + human review, final output accuracy exceeds 95%.

## Why YES (changed from NO)
The repositioning from "AI verdict" to "AI-assisted, human-validated" removes the primary commercial objection. The 60-80% time savings is well-documented across comparable domains. The model aligns with how Veeva, the FDA, and major compliance tools are deploying AI. The risk shifts from "is the AI accurate enough?" to "is the knowledge base + human review process well-executed?" — an execution challenge, not a feasibility question.
