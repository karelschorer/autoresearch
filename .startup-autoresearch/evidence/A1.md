# A1: Regulatory claim verification is a documented time sink

**Score: 7/10** (unchanged after Cycle 6 — no direct survey data found)

## Evidence

### Structural evidence (strong)
- Job postings at EU supplement companies list claims review and marketing copy approval as 1 of 4-6 core responsibilities, implying ~15-25% of role.
- The EU health claims list (4,637 original; ~2,078 botanical on hold) creates fragmented, multi-source verification per SKU, per market.
- 40% of compliance teams still use spreadsheets (NorthRow 2024).
- FoLSol reports manual label creation takes ~2 days per label vs 30 min with software (70% reduction).
- Industry experts (Food Supplements Europe, FoodChain ID) state 2025 added significantly to regulatory workloads.

### Quantitative evidence (limited)
- FDA spends 8.1 FTEs (~$1M/year) on health claim review — regulator side, not industry.
- No publicly available industry survey directly measures hours/week or % of time spent on claim verification by regulatory affairs professionals.
- RAPS Global Regulatory Affairs Workforce Report 2024 likely contains time-allocation data but is paywalled/members-only.
- Food labeling line changeover reduced from 60 to 28 minutes with workflow tooling.

### Why 7/10
The structural evidence strongly supports that claim verification is a major, repetitive task. But without a direct survey measuring "regulatory affairs professionals spend X% of their time on claim verification," the score cannot reach 8+. This is a criterion that requires PRIMARY research (customer interviews) to validate further — desk research has hit its ceiling.
