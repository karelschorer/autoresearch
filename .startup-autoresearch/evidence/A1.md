# A1: Regulatory claim verification is a documented time sink

**Score: 9/10** (improved from 8 in Cycle 18 — workflow pain now documented step-by-step)

## Evidence — "high-stakes, high-frequency failure mode with documented workflow pain"

### The manual verification workflow (Cycle 18)
The step-by-step process for each product:
1. **Identify all claims** on label, website, marketing materials (including implied claims from images)
2. **Search EU Register** by nutrient/substance — flat database with basic search, no smart matching
3. **Verify exact wording** against Regulation 432/2012 — only ~30% of products use verbatim authorized wording
4. **Cross-reference conditions of use** — minimum doses, target populations, food matrix, required accompanying statements
5. **Check botanical/on-hold claims** (~2,078 pending) — national interpretations vary by Member State
6. **Check national restrictions** per target market — melatonin dosage limits, etc.
7. **Document and sign off** — compliance dossier, label markup, cross-functional circulation

**Time per claim:** 15-45 minutes (simple vitamin/mineral), 1-3 hours (botanical/multi-market)
**Time per product:** 2-8 hours for full label compliance review
**Review capacity:** 2-5 complete labels per day per specialist
**% of RA role:** 15-30% of working time for a generalist RA role

### Non-compliance is the norm, not the exception
- **41.3% in-store, 51.2% online** non-compliant (Ulster University 2025, Ireland, 192 products)
- **89.3% non-compliant** on herbal supplements (University of Novi Sad 2024, 87 products)
- **Only 6/38 claims fully compliant** on EU protein supplements (University of Alicante 2025)
- **Only 30% use verbatim authorized wording** — wording errors are the #1 failure mode
- **85% of online supplement webpages** contain at least one false claim (Thailand 2024)

### Cost of getting it wrong
- A single labelling error costs **£100,000+ per product line** to remediate
- Average label change: ~£3,000/product; one retailer spent **£30 million** on a single compliance wave
- Errors found post-print cost **5-10x more** to fix than pre-print
- 62 products removed from EU shelves in Q1 2024 for claim violations, fines up to €500K

### The specific pain points
- **Wording precision:** Marketing teams want catchier language; RA must constantly push back. Most common failure mode.
- **Conditions of use cross-referencing:** Tedious manual comparison of formulation data vs quantitative thresholds per claim
- **Botanical limbo:** 2,078 claims on hold since 2012 with no resolution, national rules vary
- **Multi-country fragmentation:** 5 EU markets = 5 different claim assessments. No unified cross-country compliance tool from regulators.
- **EU Register UX:** Flat database with basic search. No smart matching, no paraphrase detection, no condition-of-use cross-referencing.

### Stress test findings (Cycle 18)
- **Not a one-time task:** Regulations change constantly (CJEU botanical ruling April 2025 retroactively invalidated claims on ~1,548 substances; ECGT Sept 2026; national rule changes). FoodChainID's own pitch: "running the audit...will alert you against regulatory changes."
- **FoodChainID is a more direct competitor than acknowledged:** Auto-selects compliant claims per country, multi-country audit view, regulatory change alerts. But enterprise-oriented (contact-for-quote), formulation-first (not claim-text analysis), doesn't catch paraphrases.
- **15-30% is peak-state estimate:** Steady-state probably 5-8 hrs/week. Still supports ROI at €299/mo (needs to save 3-5 hrs/mo).
- **Non-compliance may indicate some companies don't care** — but enforcement is escalating (NVWA inspections up to 36,729 in 2024 from 33,657 in 2023). ECGT will convert "don't care" companies into buyers.

### Why 9/10 (not higher)
The workflow pain is documented step-by-step with time estimates (15 min - 3 hrs per claim, 2-8 hrs per product). The non-compliance rates (41-89%) and remediation costs (£100K+) are concrete. The specific pain points map directly to ClaimPilot's features. Stress test confirmed the task is recurring (not one-time) and the ROI math works even at steady-state hours. The 9/10 ceiling: 15-30% time estimate is inferred (not surveyed), and FoodChainID is a more direct competitor than initially acknowledged. A direct customer interview would close to 10.
