# A1: Regulatory claim verification is a documented time sink

**Score: 8/10** (improved from 7 in Cycle 8 via reposition_value)

## Evidence — reframed as "high-stakes, high-frequency failure mode"

### Non-compliance is the norm, not the exception
- **41-51%** of EU supplements in-store and online carry non-compliant claims (Ireland study, Foods journal, Jan 2026)
- **59-68%** of botanical product claims fail compliance
- **~89%** of labelled claims on herbal supplements non-compliant (University of Novi Sad, 87 products)
- **100%** of "general wellbeing" claims are non-compliant — yet they're the most common category

### Cost of getting it wrong
- A single labelling error costs **£100,000+ per product line** to remediate (reprint, repackage, redistribute, plus fines)
- Average label change cost: **~£3,000/product**; one retailer spent up to **£30 million** on a single regulatory compliance wave
- Errors found post-print cost **5-10x more** to fix than pre-print errors
- 76% of companies report >10% mislabeled goods annually, averaging **$65,000 in losses**

### Insurance doesn't cover it
- Standard product liability insurance does NOT cover regulatory fines or enforcement costs
- Separate product recall insurance is rarely purchased by early-stage brands
- Influencer contracts and white-label agreements create shared liability exposure

### Time cost is real but indirect
- Typical supplement launch: 12 weeks baseline
- Post-production claim flag adds 4-8 weeks of delay (reprint, repackage, expedited shipping)
- For seasonal/trend products, a 6-week slip can mean missing the launch window entirely
- FoLSol reports manual label creation takes ~2 days per label

### Structural evidence (from Cycle 0)
- Job postings list claims review as 1 of 4-6 core duties (~15-25% of role)
- 40% of compliance teams still use spreadsheets
- Industry experts state 2025 added significantly to regulatory workloads

### Why 8/10
The reframe from "time sink" to "high-stakes failure mode" is stronger because the non-compliance rates (41-89%) and remediation costs (£100K+) are concrete and shocking. The problem is not just "this takes time" — it's "most companies are getting this wrong and the consequences are severe." The 8/10 ceiling is because the time-spent evidence remains structural/inferential rather than directly measured.
