# E2: Timing catalyst makes NOW the right moment

**Score: 8/10** (recovered from 7 in Cycle 23 — Novel Nutriology ruling replaces ECGT as timing catalyst)

## The timing case, reframed

### Strong catalysts (NL-specific, confirmed)
- **NVWA active enforcement NOW:** 6,600+ enforcement measures/year, 83% violation rate on collagen sites, Nestle/Unilever investigated. Not theoretical.
- **ACM food-sector sweep April 2025:** Warning letters sent, fines up to €900K. This is ClaimPilot's exact beachhead under enforcement pressure.
- **ECGT hard deadline September 27, 2026:** 6 months away. NL positioned for early enforcement (KLM precedent).
- **LLM maturation 2023-2024:** Technology only became viable for production regulatory use recently. This is a genuine technology timing window.

### Moderate catalysts
- PPWR packaging compliance August 12, 2026
- EFSA ongoing opinions (Feb 2026, Sept 2025, May 2025)

### Weakened catalysts (acknowledged)
- GCD withdrawn June 2025 — shows political flexibility
- Botanical claims perpetually "imminent" (not a NOW argument)
- Simplification Omnibus could reduce future regulatory complexity
- ECGT enforcement will be patchy outside NL

### ECGT conflation (Cycle 22 — critical correction)
- **ECGT (EU 2024/825) is about sustainability/environmental claims** — banning unsubstantiated "eco-friendly," "climate neutral," etc.
- **ECGT does NOT change health claims regulation (EC 1924/2006)** — health claims compliance has no imminent deadline
- The ECGT deadline (Sept 2026) may indirectly increase compliance budgets/awareness, but cannot be used as a direct demand catalyst for health claims tools
- ClaimPilot's wedge (C2) includes ECGT sustainability claims pre-screening, so ECGT IS relevant for that feature — but the primary health claims use case has no deadline urgency

### Novel Nutriology ruling — the real timing catalyst (Cycle 23)
- **CJEU Case C-386/23 (April 30, 2025):** Ruled that botanical health claims are prohibited unless on the approved list or properly filed under transitional provisions. Kills the decade-long "on-hold" loophole.
- **Impact:** Any company using botanical claims without proper transitional filing is NOW in violation. Botanicals are the fastest-growing supplement segment. This affects a huge portion of the market.
- **Industry reaction:** Described as "a lose-lose situation" creating "urgent need for NHCR reform." This is the health claims equivalent of ECGT — a recent, binding legal change that creates immediate compliance risk.

### Marketplace gating — non-regulatory timing catalyst
- **Amazon EU:** Now requires third-party compliance verification from NSF/UL/Eurofins. Suppresses listings without verification. Health claims must be backed by scientific evidence.
- **Bol.com:** Explicit policies prohibiting poorly substantiated claims. Will delist non-compliant products. Melatonin restrictions.
- **Impact:** Revenue threat (delisting) is a stronger purchase trigger than fine threat for many brands.

### Why 8/10
The timing case is rebuilt on stronger foundations: (1) CJEU Novel Nutriology ruling (April 2025) kills the botanical on-hold loophole — genuine, recent, binding; (2) NVWA enforcement broadening to thematic sweeps and influencers; (3) marketplace gating (Amazon/Bol.com) creates revenue-threatening compliance pressure; (4) LLM technology maturation 2023-2024. The ECGT deadline adds a secondary catalyst for sustainability claims specifically. The 8/10 ceiling: enforcement probability for any specific brand remains ~5%/year, and the Novel Nutriology ruling may trigger regulatory reform that simplifies the landscape.
