# E2: Timing catalyst makes NOW the right moment

**Score: 7/10** (dropped from 9 after Cycle 13 stress test)

## Evidence — catalysts are real but less certain than initially scored

### Strong catalysts (confirmed)
- ECGT enforcement September 27, 2026 — hard deadline, no delay
- Dutch ACM enforcement letters April 2025 — active in beachhead market
- LLM technology maturation — only viable for production regulatory use since 2023-2024

### Weakened catalysts

**ECGT enforcement will be inconsistent:**
- National authorities enforce variably — Germany/France aggressive, others lag
- GDPR precedent: early enforcement was patchy for years before ramping up
- Supplement brand claim enforcement is lower-profile than aviation/fashion greenwashing
- Commission FAQ leaves "open questions" — creates enforcement uncertainty

**Green Claims Directive withdrawal:**
- Originally positioned as a key catalyst — now withdrawn (June 2025)
- ECGT fills most of the gap but the withdrawal signals political flexibility
- The regulatory trajectory is not as linear as initially scored

**Botanical claims are not a timing catalyst:**
- On hold since 2012 — 14 years with no resolution
- Cannot be used as a "NOW" argument when the trigger is perpetually deferred

**Simplification Omnibus is a counter-catalyst:**
- If enacted, could reduce the regulatory complexity that drives demand for ClaimPilot
- Heads to Parliament plenary March 2026

### Why 7/10
The ECGT hard deadline (6 months away) and Dutch ACM enforcement are genuine, time-bound catalysts. LLM maturation is a real technology timing window. But the thesis was overconfident at 9/10: the GCD withdrawal, enforcement patchiness, perpetual botanical limbo, and Simplification Omnibus all reduce the urgency and certainty. The NOW window is real but narrower and less robust than initially scored.
