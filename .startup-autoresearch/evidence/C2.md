# C2: Credible narrow wedge for supplements

**Score: 8/10** (dropped from 9 after Cycle 10 stress test, partially recovered by post-flag workflow addition)

## Evidence

### The wedge IS separable (for SMEs)
- **Apex Compliance** exists as a standalone claim-vetting tool and found paying customers including Allergy Research Group. Tim Gerke called it "affordable and perfect for small and medium-sized startups that can't afford a full regulatory department."
- **Signify** and **Trustwell** started as narrow label/claim tools before expanding — validating the point solution → platform progression.
- SMEs (ClaimPilot's real ICP) do buy point solutions for specific compliance tasks.

### Supplements are the highest-density use case
- EU supplement products carry 6-7 health claims per product (up to 12 for some categories)
- Compliance rate only 48.8-58.7% — massive unmet need
- The market is large ($87.33B Europe, 2024) and companies pay per-product fees to consultants

### Stress test findings — weaknesses identified
- **Post-flag workflow gap:** When a claim is flagged, users need to know what to do next (reformulate? relabel? what CAN they say?). Without "suggested compliant alternatives," ClaimPilot solves ~20% of the job-to-be-done.
- **Platform pressure:** Assent's analysis states "point solutions cannot scale with your requirements." Larger companies with Veeva/MasterControl won't adopt a point tool.
- **Ceiling exists:** The standalone wedge works for entry but faces a ceiling as companies grow. Successful point solutions (SafetyChain) eventually face platform pressure.

### Fix applied (Cycle 10 mutation)
- Added "post-flag workflow" to model: when a claim is flagged non-compliant, ClaimPilot suggests compliant alternative wordings from the authorized EU Register matching the product's ingredients and dosage.
- This closes the job-to-be-done without becoming a full PLM platform.
- Thin "next step" layer keeps users in the tool and increases retention.

### Why 8/10
The wedge is real for SMEs but has a ceiling. The post-flag workflow partially addresses the biggest weakness (incomplete job-to-be-done). The platform pressure risk is real but is a year-2-3 problem, not a year-1 problem. For the beachhead (Dutch supplement SMEs, 50-300 SKUs), the point solution is sufficient.
