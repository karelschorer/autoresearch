# C2: Credible narrow wedge for supplements

**Score: 9/10** (recovered from 8 in Cycle 11 via ride_catalyst — ECGT expansion)

## Evidence (updated Cycle 11)

### Combined health + sustainability claim verification is a strong wedge
- **ECGT adds a second compliance clock on the same packaging.** Supplement brands using sustainability/natural positioning (70%+ of consumers prefer eco-branded products) now need to verify both health claims (EC 1924/2006) AND sustainability claims (ECGT) — from September 27, 2026.
- **No existing tool covers both.** General ESG platforms (Arbor, Zevero) handle carbon data, not claim-level pre-screening. No tool addresses the intersection of food/supplement marketing copy + ECGT compliance.
- **Combining creates lock-in:** The tool becomes embedded in the brand's FULL claims workflow (health + sustainability), not just one part. Justifies higher pricing and increases switching costs.
- **ECGT bans common claims:** "eco-friendly," "green," "sustainable," "natural," "biodegradable" all require substantiation. "Carbon neutral" claims based on offsets are banned outright. Partial claims (one sustainable ingredient ≠ "sustainable product") are prohibited.

### The supplement wedge is self-contained (with fixes)
- EU supplements carry 6-7 health claims per product (up to 12)
- Compliance rate only 48.8-58.7%
- Pre-screening is a discrete, front-loaded task before production
- Apex Compliance validated standalone claim tools with paying SME customers
- **Post-flag workflow added:** Suggest compliant alternative wordings from EU Register. Closes the job-to-be-done.

### Remaining ceiling
- Platform pressure: larger companies with Veeva/MasterControl won't adopt a point tool (year 2-3 problem)
- The wedge works for SMEs at beachhead scale but may need platform evolution to retain growing customers

### Why 9/10
The ECGT expansion makes the wedge significantly stronger — same customer, same packaging, two regulatory clocks, no existing tool covers both. The post-flag workflow fixes the biggest weakness (incomplete job-to-be-done). The remaining ceiling (platform pressure at scale) is a year 2-3 problem, not a year 1 problem.
