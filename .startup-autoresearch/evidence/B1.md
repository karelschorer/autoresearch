# B1: Existing alternatives are inadequate for mid-market

**Score: 9/10** (improved from 8 in Cycle 9 — direct workflow evidence)

## Evidence (updated Cycle 9)

### The manual reality is confirmed by industry insiders
- **Rubicon Bridge** (compliance tech company) explicitly describes mid-size supplement companies using "comprehensive spreadsheets cataloging permitted substances, allowable usage levels, and mandatory warnings" as the **standard approach**. Notes it is "labor-intensive, susceptible to errors."
- The EU Commission provides the Register as a **downloadable Excel file** — confirming this is the workflow regulators assume companies use.

### Named tools that exist — and why they miss (updated Cycle 19)
| Tool | What it does | Why it misses |
|---|---|---|
| Veeva Vault RIM | Pharma registration tracking | Min ~$50K/year; not for food claims |
| SAP EHS | Enterprise ERP compliance | Tier 1 ERP, not for mid-market |
| FoodChainID/Nutraveris | **Formulation-in, claims-out** label generation | Enterprise-tier, contact-for-quote. Does NOT analyze marketing copy — works opposite direction |
| Apex Compliance | **Keyword scanning** of marketing content for risky terms | **US-only** (FDA/FTC). No EU health claims database. Keyword matching, not semantic analysis |
| Trustwell/AskReg | AI regulatory Q&A chatbot | Not a document scanner. Cannot ingest marketing materials for bulk screening |
| SGS DigiComply (Labelwise) | Label artwork validation from images | Enterprise-only. Health claims is secondary. No marketing copy analysis |
| Rubicon Bridge | Formulation compliance + claims assignment | Formulation-first (like FoodChainID). Does not analyze claim text |
| Ecomundo | EU supplement registration + consulting | Consulting model (by-the-hour) |
| CIKLab | QC/batch tracking for nutraceuticals | €47/mo but solves QC, not claims |

### Critical gap validated: NO tool does AI-powered claim-TEXT pre-screening for EU
- **FoodChainID + Apex partnership** is the strongest signal: the market leader in formulation compliance had to partner with a SEPARATE tool for marketing content review — and even that partner is US-only with keyword matching, no EU coverage
- No existing tool can: (1) take arbitrary marketing copy, (2) use NLP to identify paraphrased health claims, (3) cross-reference against EU Register, (4) assess if paraphrased wording "has the same meaning for consumers" (the legal standard under EC 1924/2006)
- The UK ASA has ruled against claims that simply removed the word "normal" from authorized wording — this level of semantic precision requires NLP, not keyword lists
- The EU Register is public data, but the interpretation layer (paraphrasing rules, conditions of use, botanical on-hold status, member state variations) is where ClaimPilot creates value

### Consultancy rates are prohibitive for ongoing use
- Senior EU regulatory consultants: $225-300/hour on freelance platforms (Kolabtree)
- Mid-level food regulatory consultants: €75-150/hour
- At 15-20 hours/month of claim verification, consultancy costs €1,125-3,000/month

### Why 9/10
Deep competitive analysis (Cycle 19) confirmed: NO existing tool does AI-powered claim-text pre-screening for EU health claims. FoodChainID is formulation-first (opposite direction), Apex is US-only keyword matching, Trustwell is Q&A, SGS/Rubicon are enterprise/formulation. The FoodChainID+Apex partnership validates the gap exists. The 9/10 ceiling: adjacent tools could add claim pre-screening as a feature, and Apex could expand to EU. But the semantic analysis of paraphrased claims is a genuine technical moat.
