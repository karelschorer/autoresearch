# D1: >=100 identifiable target companies reachable through <=2 channels

**Score: 8/10** (recovered from 7 in Cycle 14 via GTM reposition)

## The companies exist (confirmed)
- NPN: 100-104 Dutch member companies
- EHPM: 1,600+ EU health-product manufacturers (90% SMEs)
- Vitafoods 2025: 1,455 exhibitors, ~580 in ICP

## Conversion friction is real (Cycle 12 stress test)
- Cold outreach: 3-5% reply, 1-2% meeting-booked
- NPN list gated to non-members
- CAC: $2,000-3,000 early stage
- Sales cycle: 3-6 months

## Fixed GTM (Cycle 14)
1. **ECGT deadline inbound:** Free "ECGT Compliance Checker" drives self-qualified leads (GDPR analogue proven)
2. **Consultant partnerships:** White-label/referral with Dutch consultancies. Referral leads convert at 24.7% MQL-to-SQL
3. **Trade association co-marketing:** Sponsor NPN/FSE webinars for access to membership without cold outreach ($500-5,000)
4. **PLG freemium:** 10 claims/month free tier. Median conversion 9-12%
5. **Founder network:** Stijn's warm introductions for first 5-10 design partners

## Stress test findings (Cycle 22 — severe)
- **ECGT inbound is fatally flawed:** ECGT (EU 2024/825) covers sustainability/environmental claims, NOT health claims. No deadline-driven urgency exists for health claims compliance. The "ECGT Compliance Checker" as health claims inbound tool is a regulatory mismatch.
- **Consultant partnerships face revenue cannibalization:** ClaimPilot automates the core deliverable of Dutch regulatory consultancies (claim checking against EU Register). Expecting referrals from firms whose billable hours you directly threaten is unrealistic.
- **NPN has no SaaS vendor membership:** NPN requires manufacturer/supplier/distributor membership. No technology vendor category. Trade bodies don't endorse specific vendors.
- **PLG free tier contradicts compliance trust:** No compliance SaaS (Wolters Kluwer, NAVEX, Credo AI) uses freemium. Free = untrustworthy for high-stakes regulatory decisions.
- **TAM likely 30-70 brands** at 50-300 SKU scale (not 80-150) after filtering out suppliers, distributors, micro-brands. NL has no supplement notification system making exact counts impossible.

## What still works
- Founder network for first 5-10 design partners (validated, time-limited)
- Content marketing around NVWA enforcement actions (not ECGT, but genuine urgency)
- Direct outreach to brands found non-compliant in NVWA sweeps (public enforcement data)
- Product-led trial (not free tier — time-limited trial with expert support)

## Why 7/10
Five GTM channels, each with structural problems. The ECGT conflation is fatal for the primary inbound mechanism. Consultant partnerships face revenue cannibalization. NPN access is speculative. PLG free tier contradicts compliance trust. TAM is likely half of initial estimate. What remains: founder network (5-10 intros), NVWA-enforcement-driven content marketing, and direct outreach. These are viable but narrower and slower than originally positioned.
