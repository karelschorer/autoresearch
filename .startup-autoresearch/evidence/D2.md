# D2: Unit economics work — value >=3x price

**Score: 5/10** (dropped from 8 after Cycle 3 stress test)

## Original evidence (Cycle 0)

- Regulatory affairs manager salary NL: €65,000-€96,000/year. All-in cost: ~€50-75/hour.
- At 15-20 hours/month saved, value = €750-€1,500/month — 2.5-5x the €300-500 price.
- NVWA fines: €525-€1,050 per violation; up to €150,000 for medicinal misclassification.
- Packaging non-compliance: €5,000-50,000+ per redesign event.

## Stress test findings (Cycle 3) — PRICING MODEL BREAKS

**The managed service model at €300-500/month doesn't work at scale:**
- At 200 SKUs × 6-7 claims = 1,200-1,400 claims per customer
- AI handles 80% → 240-280 claims need human review
- At 5 min/claim, that's 20-23 hours of expert review per cycle
- At €83/hr freelance rate: €1,660-1,900 per review cycle per customer
- At €45-50/hr employed rate: €900-1,150 per review cycle per customer
- €300-500/month subscription doesn't cover labor costs even with AI handling 80%

**Comparable managed compliance services charge much more:**
- VComply: ~$1,000/month
- Regology: ~$1,250/user/month
- Break-even for genuine expert review at 200 SKUs: €1,500-3,000/month minimum

**The €300-500/month pricing only works if:**
- (a) "expert review" is a cursory spot-check, not full validation
- (b) Target customers have <30-50 SKUs
- (c) AI accuracy is near-100% and expert review is minimal
- (d) It's a deliberate loss-leader for land-and-expand

## Why 5/10
The VALUE delivered is real and significant (time saved + risk avoided). But the PRICING doesn't cover the COST of delivering the promised service. The unit economics have a cost-side problem, not a value-side problem. This is fixable through pricing restructuring.
