# D2: Unit economics work — value >=3x price

**Score: 8/10** (Cycle 17: fine-avoidance ROI articulated then stress-tested back to 8)

## Tiered pricing model (Cycle 4)

### Cost structure per tier:
- **Free:** Near-zero marginal cost. Data acquisition only.
- **Starter (€299/mo):** AI compute costs only (~€10-20/mo per customer). ~95% gross margin.
- **Professional (€799/mo):** AI + 10% spot-check. At 200 SKUs × 7 claims × 20% flagged × 10% spot-check = 28 claims × 10 min = 5 hrs × €50/hr = €250 cost. **69% gross margin.**
- **Enterprise (€2-5K/mo):** Full expert review priced at sustainable rates. At €2K/mo with 20-25 hrs expert time = €1,000-1,250 cost. **37-50% gross margin** (acceptable for managed service).

## Total cost of non-compliance ROI model (Cycle 17)

### NVWA enforcement reality:
- **Per-violation fines are low:** €525 (<50 employees) to €1,050 (>50 employees) under food law
- **But fines stack:** Multiple violations per product/inspection. One case: online drugstore fined >€73K (many violations)
- **55% non-compliance rate** across food businesses for health claims (NVWA data)
- **75-85% non-compliance** in themed sweeps (collagen 83%, horse milk 83%, weight-loss 78%)
- 75% of inspections are unannounced

### The real cost is INDIRECT (not fines):
- **Packaging redesign + inventory write-off:** €10,000-100,000 depending on SKU count
- **Legal counsel for enforcement response:** €5,000-25,000
- **Relabeling delays:** 4-8 weeks lost sales
- **Reputational damage:** 55% of consumers switch brands after a recall; 21% never buy from manufacturer again
- **Ponemon benchmark:** Non-compliance costs **2.71x** the cost of maintaining compliance

### Revised ROI model per tier:
| Component | Conservative | Moderate |
|---|---|---|
| Annual enforcement probability | 15% | 25% |
| Expected fine per action | €2,000 | €10,000 |
| Indirect costs (relabeling, legal, inventory) | €25,000 | €100,000 |
| **Total expected annual loss** | **€4,050** | **€27,500** |

- **Starter (€3,588/yr):** 1.1-7.7x ROI on fine avoidance alone + time savings (5-10 hrs/mo = €3,000-7,500/yr)
- **Professional (€9,588/yr):** 0.4-2.9x ROI on fines + time savings (15-20 hrs/mo = €9,000-15,000/yr). Combined: **1.4-4.7x**
- **Enterprise (€24-60K/yr):** 0.2-1.1x on fines + time savings (30-40 hrs/mo = €18,000-30,000/yr). Combined: **0.9-2.4x**

### The sales message that works:
"Your compliance costs €X with us, or non-compliance costs 2.71x that without us. And with 55% industry non-compliance, the question is when, not if."

### Comparable pricing validates range:
- VComply: ~$1,000/month
- Regology: ~$1,250/user/month
- Deloitte benchmark: $250-$2,000/user/month for regulatory software

### Stress test weaknesses (Cycle 17)
- **Enforcement probability overstated:** Realistic annual inspection probability ~5% (not 15-25%). NVWA oversees 250K food businesses, inspects ~16K/yr total, supplement-specific sweeps are small (30-50 businesses)
- **Ponemon 2.71x is a category error:** 53 US multinationals in cybersecurity ≠ Dutch SME supplement brands. Should not cite as benchmark.
- **Freelance consultant substitute:** €2-4K one-time review for 50 SKUs undercuts Starter tier (€3.6K/yr). Neutralized by SKU churn, ECGT-driven ongoing re-review, and speed of individual checks.
- **Honest ROI:** With 5% enforcement probability, expected annual loss ~€1-1.8K. Combined with time savings (€3-7.5K/yr Starter, €9-15K/yr Pro), total ROI is 1.1-2.6x Starter, 1.0-1.7x Professional. Below 3x target.

### Why 8/10 (not higher)
The total cost of non-compliance data is useful but the ROI framing was overconfident. Honest ROI is 1-2.5x (not 1.4-7.7x). Time savings remain the primary value driver and are deterministic. The 8/10 acknowledges: (a) Starter tier value/price is tight, (b) freelance consultant is a real substitute for low-churn brands, (c) fine avoidance is secondary to time savings. To reach 9+: need customer validation of willingness to pay at these price points.
