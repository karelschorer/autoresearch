## Top 3 Recommendations

### #1: MiCAGuard — AI-Powered MiCA Compliance (72/100)

**Why it wins:** Highest E1 score (9/10) — Jeroen's Kraken exit and crypto network is an unfair advantage that no other team can replicate. MiCA is the strongest timing catalyst on the list: mandatory regulation, just became effective, technical standards still being published. Karel builds the AI pipeline. The team-market fit is exceptional.

**Biggest risk:** TAM. EU MiCA-licensed entities number in the hundreds, not thousands. The beachhead (Dutch DNB-registered firms, ~40) is small. Needs international expansion to DE/FR/rest of EU quickly. Also, the competitive landscape (Chainalysis, Sumsub, Notabene) covers adjacent compliance needs — the specific gap is MiCA marketing compliance + regulatory Q&A.

**Cycle 1 hypothesis:** The TAM concern (D1: 5/10) is the weakest criterion. First mutation should be `narrow_icp` or `adjust_economics` — define exactly which type of MiCA-licensed entity (CASPs, stablecoin issuers, token issuers) has the most acute compliance pain and how many there are across the EU. Web research: count MiCA license applications per jurisdiction, map the compliance tooling landscape, find the specific gap no tool fills.

### #2: RegRadar — Regulatory Change Intelligence (71/100)

**Why it's strong:** Largest addressable market (D1: 7/10, thousands of EU financial institutions). Strongest problem-growth signal (A2: 9/10 — Europe's regulatory supercycle is real and structural). Clean wedge (C2: 8/10 — AI-generated impact alerts). Both founders understand fintech. Recurring revenue, low churn (compliance teams can't stop tracking regulations).

**Biggest risk:** Crowded RegTech market. Thomson Reuters, Wolters Kluwer, Cube, Corlytics are all here. The mid-market gap (too small for TR, too complex for manual) is real but narrowing as competitors move downmarket. E1 (7/10) is good but not exceptional — no proprietary domain expertise, would need to hire or partner for regulatory depth.

**Cycle 1 hypothesis:** The competitive landscape (B1: 6/10, B2: 6/10) needs stress-testing. First research: map every RegTech competitor serving European mid-market financial institutions. Identify the specific gap — is it price (cheaper than TR), scope (fintech-specific), or capability (AI impact assessment)? Mutation: `narrow_icp` to Dutch fintechs/payment providers specifically, or `shift_wedge` to MiCA/DORA change monitoring specifically (leveraging Jeroen's crypto domain).

### #3: VaultOps — Crypto Treasury Management (68/100)

**Why it's interesting:** Most balanced scores (no criterion below 5 except D1 and D2). Clear gap in the market (B1: 7/10, B2: 7/10) — neither crypto custody tools nor enterprise treasury tools serve this use case. Strong team fit (E1: 8/10) — Jeroen literally built crypto data infrastructure. Multiple timing catalysts (E2: 8/10) — MiCA governance + institutional adoption + market recovery.

**Biggest risk:** Dual weakness in D1 (TAM: 5/10) and D2 (willingness-to-pay: 5/10). Crypto firms with significant treasuries are few and cost-sensitive. The EUR 499/mo Starter tier may compete with "we use spreadsheets" inertia. Market size depends on crypto market health.

**Cycle 1 hypothesis:** The economics (D1 + D2) need validation. Research: how many MiCA-licensed entities have treasury operations above EUR 5M? What do crypto firms currently spend on treasury tooling? Are CFOs at crypto firms willing to pay for SaaS treasury management? Mutation: `adjust_economics` — potentially shift from SaaS to value-based pricing (% of AUM or per-transaction) if the SaaS model doesn't justify.

## Pattern Analysis

**What works for Karel + Jeroen:**
- Crypto/fintech domain (E1 scores 7-9 for crypto ideas vs. 4-6 for non-crypto)
- Compliance/regulatory angle (A2 scores 7-9 when regulatory pressure exists)
- Data product plays (both are data product builders)

**What doesn't work:**
- Enterprise with long sales cycles (airline, large enterprise: D1 scores 3-4)
- Crowded horizontal markets (eval tools, financial doc AI: B1 scores 4-5)
- Ideas where neither founder has domain expertise (pricing, airline ops: E1 scores 4-5)

**The recurring challenge:** TAM. Their strongest ideas (crypto compliance, crypto treasury) have small addressable markets. Their largest-TAM idea (RegRadar) has less team fit. This is the core tension to resolve.
