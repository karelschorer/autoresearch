# DealLens — Scores (Cycle 0 Baseline)

**Total: 64/100**

## A1: Problem is real — 8/10
Due diligence is genuinely painful, expensive, and time-consuming. EUR 50-100K+ per deal, 4-8 weeks, hundreds of documents. Every PE fund, M&A advisor, and corporate BD team feels this pain. Well-documented problem with clear costs. The pain is real and recurring for active dealmakers.

## A2: Problem is growing — 6/10
M&A volume fluctuates with market cycles. European M&A was down in 2023-2024, recovering in 2025-2026. The problem grows with deal volume, not with regulatory or structural changes. Moderate and cyclical growth.

## B1: Alternatives inadequate — 5/10
Luminance, Kira Systems (now Litera), and Eigen Technologies already offer AI-powered document analysis for M&A. Datasite and Intralinks are adding AI features to their VDR platforms. Harvey (AI for legal) can assist with diligence. The market has multiple funded players. The "unified diligence across financial + legal + operational" angle is somewhat novel but each vertical has established players.

## B2: Gap exists — 6/10
The "holistic diligence" gap is real — no tool connects financial, legal, and operational analysis into one workflow. But building this requires depth in all three domains, which is a massive undertaking. The gap exists partly because it's genuinely hard to fill, not just because no one has tried.

## C1: Technology can deliver — 7/10
LLMs are good at document analysis and summarization. The technology works for individual documents. Cross-document analysis and risk pattern detection is more challenging. Accuracy requirements are high — missing a material risk in diligence has serious consequences. Technically feasible with careful engineering.

## C2: Wedge works — 7/10
Financial due diligence automation (extract + cross-reference financial metrics) is a clear, bounded wedge with standalone value. Saves the most junior analyst time. Good entry point before expanding to legal/operational diligence.

## D1: Market reachable — 6/10
European PE/VC funds and M&A advisors: several hundred identifiable targets. Reachable through financial community networks and events. But deal teams are busy, skeptical of new tools mid-deal, and slow to adopt. Sales cycles are long.

## D2: Unit economics — 7/10
Per-deal pricing (EUR 2-5K) aligns with buyer economics — a deal that costs EUR 50K in manual diligence can easily justify EUR 5K for automation. Clear 10x value proposition. But per-deal pricing means lumpy revenue, not recurring SaaS.

## E1: Team advantage — 6/10
Jeroen has been through a due diligence process (as seller, not buyer). That's useful context but not deep domain expertise. Neither founder has PE/M&A/advisory background. Karel's AI skills are relevant but generic. No unique network into the PE/M&A world. Moderate fit.

## E2: Timing catalyst — 6/10
AI capability improvement makes this possible now (vs. 2 years ago). European M&A recovery in 2025-2026 increases deal volume. But no sharp deadline or regulatory event. "AI is better now" is a general trend, not a specific catalyst.
