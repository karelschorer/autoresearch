# DealLens — AI-Powered Due Diligence for M&A

## Problem Statement
M&A due diligence is a EUR 50-100K+ process per deal that takes 4-8 weeks. Analysts review hundreds of documents (contracts, financials, IP, compliance, HR) looking for risks, inconsistencies, and red flags. Most of this is reading and cross-referencing — exactly what LLMs excel at. Current VDR platforms (Intralinks, Datasite) provide document storage but zero intelligence. Legal tech tools (Kira, Luminance) focus on contract analysis, not holistic deal diligence. The gap: no tool connects financial, legal, operational, and compliance diligence into a unified AI-powered workflow.

## Target Customer (ICP)
PE/VC funds, corporate M&A teams, and M&A advisory boutiques in Europe doing 5-50 deals/year. Beachhead: Dutch and DACH PE funds and M&A advisors. These teams have high deal volume, time pressure, and recurring document review patterns.

## Proposed Solution & Wedge
AI platform that ingests a full data room, automatically categorizes documents, extracts key terms/risks/flags, and generates a structured diligence report with citations. Human reviewers focus on judgment calls, not document reading. Wedge: automated financial due diligence — extract and cross-reference financial metrics across years of filings, flag inconsistencies, generate financial summary with red flags.

## Pricing Model
- Per-deal pricing: EUR 2-5K per deal (variable with data room size)
- Annual license: EUR 25-50K/year for high-volume users (>10 deals/year)
- White-label for advisory firms: custom pricing
