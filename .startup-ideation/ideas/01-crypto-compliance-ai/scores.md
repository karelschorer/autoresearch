# MiCAGuard — Scores (Cycle 0 Baseline)

**Total: 72/100**

## A1: Problem is real — 8/10
MiCA compliance is mandatory and new. Every EU crypto firm must comply or lose its license. Compliance teams are small and overwhelmed by novel regulation. The pain is documented and urgent — DNB/BaFin are actively processing license applications. Not a 9 because the crypto industry is small and some firms are outsourcing to law firms rather than building internal compliance.

## A2: Problem is growing — 8/10
MiCA just became fully effective (Jan 2026). Level 2 technical standards are still being published by ESMA throughout 2026. The regulatory surface is literally expanding in real-time. Additional regulations (TFR for travel rule, DORA for operational resilience) compound the burden. Strong growth trajectory.

## B1: Alternatives inadequate — 6/10
Big Four consultancies serve this market at high prices, but several RegTech startups are already targeting MiCA (Notabene for travel rule, Chainalysis for transaction monitoring, Sumsub for KYC). The compliance tooling market for crypto is more developed than it first appears. The specific "MiCA marketing compliance" wedge may be underserved, but the broader space is crowded.

## B2: Gap exists — 6/10
The marketing compliance angle is somewhat novel, but MiCA marketing rules are simpler than EU health claims (essentially: be fair, clear, not misleading, include risk warnings). A generic LLM could handle most marketing review. The gap is narrower than it looks — the hard MiCA compliance problems (reserve management, prudential requirements) are not what this tool addresses.

## C1: Technology can deliver — 8/10
MiCA regulation text is well-structured and finite. LLM-based regulatory Q&A and document generation is a proven pattern (see: Harvey for legal, Casetext for case law). Karel can build this. The marketing compliance checker is technically straightforward — the regulation is clear about what's required.

## C2: Wedge works — 7/10
Marketing compliance scanning is a real workflow but may not be high-frequency enough to justify a monthly subscription. Crypto firms update marketing materials quarterly, not daily. The wedge might be too narrow for standalone value. Could expand to disclosure generation but that's a bigger product.

## D1: Market reachable — 5/10
~40 DNB-registered crypto firms in NL. Even expanding to DE/FR, the total MiCA-licensed entity count in EU is still small (hundreds, not thousands) in 2026. The >=100 target threshold is borderline. Reachable through crypto communities and Jeroen's network, but the TAM is small.

## D2: Unit economics — 7/10
At EUR 299-999/mo, the value proposition is clear vs. EUR 500/hr Big Four rates. A crypto firm spending EUR 50K+ on MiCA compliance advisory could justify EUR 12K/yr SaaS easily. But willingness-to-pay is unproven — crypto firms are cost-conscious and many are struggling post-bear-market.

## E1: Team advantage — 9/10
Jeroen sold a company to Kraken. He knows how crypto firms operate, what they care about, and who makes buying decisions. Karel builds AI products. This is the ideal team for crypto compliance AI — domain insider + AI builder. Jeroen's network provides warm intros to the exact buyer persona. Very strong team fit.

## E2: Timing catalyst — 8/10
MiCA full effectiveness (Jan 2026) is the catalyst. ESMA technical standards still being published. The compliance burden is peaking right now. However, this is a one-time compliance wave — once firms are compliant, ongoing monitoring is lower-effort. The timing window is strong but finite.
