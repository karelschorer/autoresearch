# EvalForge — Scores (Cycle 0 Baseline)

**Total: 62/100**

## A1: Problem is real — 7/10
Every team deploying LLMs needs evaluation. The pain is real but varies widely — some teams build custom eval in a few days, others struggle for months. The problem exists but is not uniformly severe. Many teams consider eval "nice to have" until a production failure forces the issue.

## A2: Problem is growing — 8/10
LLM adoption in enterprise is accelerating rapidly. EU AI Act creates specific requirements for model testing, bias detection, and documentation. The problem is clearly growing as more companies move LLMs to production. Multiple analyst reports confirm AI governance is a top enterprise priority.

## B1: Alternatives inadequate — 5/10
This space is getting crowded. Braintrust, LangSmith, Weights & Biases, Arize, and others offer LLM evaluation features. Open-source options (Ragas, DeepEval, LangChain eval) are maturing fast. The differentiator would need to be EU AI Act compliance focus, but Credo AI and Holistic AI already target AI governance. Hard to find a clear gap.

## B2: Gap exists — 5/10
The specific gap (regulated-industry eval templates with AI Act compliance reports) is niche but real. However, existing platforms are rapidly adding governance features. The window for differentiation is closing as the market matures. Building "another eval platform" is a red ocean.

## C1: Technology can deliver — 8/10
Karel has literally built eval harnesses. The technology is proven and within his existing skillset. LLM evaluation is well-understood technically. The challenge isn't "can we build it" but "can we build something meaningfully better than existing options."

## C2: Wedge works — 6/10
EU AI Act compliance reporting could be a wedge, but it requires deep regulatory knowledge that neither founder has. The wedge is adjacent to their skills, not core. Pre-built industry eval templates require domain expertise in each industry (banking, healthcare, legal) — hard to build credibly without domain partners.

## D1: Market reachable — 7/10
Enterprises deploying LLMs in EU are numerous and growing. Reachable through developer communities, AI conferences, and Karel's enterprise network. >100 targets easily identifiable. But competition for their attention is fierce — every AI tooling company is marketing to the same buyers.

## D2: Unit economics — 6/10
At EUR 199-799/mo, the tool competes with free open-source alternatives and VC-funded competitors offering generous free tiers. The AI Act compliance angle could command premium pricing but needs to be proven. Value quantification is hard — "better eval" doesn't translate directly to EUR saved.

## E1: Team advantage — 6/10
Karel has eval harness experience, which is genuine. But this is increasingly commodity knowledge — thousands of ML engineers build eval pipelines. Jeroen's fintech/crypto background doesn't add much to an LLM eval platform. Team fit is moderate, not exceptional. No unique domain advantage.

## E2: Timing catalyst — 4/10
EU AI Act's high-risk requirements phase in gradually (2025-2027), but the urgency is low — enforcement is unclear, implementation is slow, and most companies are in "wait and see" mode. No sharp deadline. The AI eval market has been growing for 2+ years without a clear inflection point.
