# RegRadar — AI-Powered Regulatory Change Intelligence for Financial Services

## Problem Statement
Financial institutions (banks, insurers, asset managers, payment providers) must track and respond to regulatory changes across multiple jurisdictions. A mid-size European bank tracks 50-100 regulatory bodies, processes 2,000-5,000 regulatory updates per year, and must assess impact on policies, products, and operations. This is done by compliance teams of 5-20 people reading alerts, PDFs, and consultation papers. Missing a change means non-compliance. Current tools (Thomson Reuters Regulatory Intelligence, Wolters Kluwer) are expensive (EUR 50-100K+/year), document-heavy, and require manual impact assessment.

## Target Customer (ICP)
Mid-size European financial institutions: payment providers, neobanks, crypto firms (MiCA), insurers, asset managers, and fintech companies with 50-500 employees. Too large for manual tracking, too small for EUR 100K+ enterprise tools. Beachhead: Dutch fintechs and payment providers (DNB/AFM-regulated).

## Proposed Solution & Wedge
AI platform that monitors regulatory sources (central banks, ESAs, national regulators), classifies changes by topic/jurisdiction/entity type, assesses relevance to the customer's specific license and products, and generates impact assessments with action items. Wedge: automated regulatory change alerting with AI-generated impact summary — "this ECB guideline change affects your payment license because X, action required by Y date."

## Pricing Model
- Starter (EUR 399/mo): 5 regulatory bodies monitored, weekly digest, basic impact assessment
- Professional (EUR 1,499/mo): 20+ regulators, real-time alerts, custom classification, compliance dashboard
- Enterprise (EUR 5-10K/mo): Unlimited sources, policy mapping, audit trail, API integration

## Go-to-Market
Holland FinTech network. Dutch fintech/payment community (Amsterdam). Direct outreach to Chief Compliance Officers. Compliance conference sponsorships. Content marketing: weekly regulatory digest newsletter as lead gen.

## Defensibility & Moat
Regulatory source coverage is cumulative (each new regulator/jurisdiction added improves the product for all). Customer-specific classifications improve the AI over time (data flywheel).
