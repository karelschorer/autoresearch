# RegRadar — Scores (Cycle 0 Baseline)

**Total: 71/100**

## A1: Problem is real — 8/10
Regulatory change management is a documented, significant cost center. A mid-size bank's compliance team spends 30-40% of time tracking and assessing regulatory changes. Thomson Reuters estimates 200+ regulatory changes per day globally. The pain is well-documented in compliance officer surveys and consulting reports. Real, recurring, and expensive.

## A2: Problem is growing — 9/10
Regulatory surface is expanding rapidly: MiCA (crypto), DORA (operational resilience), PSD3 (payments), AI Act, AML Package, CSRD. Europe is in a regulatory supercycle. Every financial institution is dealing with more regulations, more jurisdictions, more complexity. Strong, structural growth.

## B1: Alternatives inadequate — 6/10
Thomson Reuters Regulatory Intelligence and Wolters Kluwer are established players but expensive (EUR 50-100K+/year). They serve large banks well but mid-market (fintechs, payment providers, smaller banks) finds them overpriced and overkill. Cube, Corlytics, and CUBE are newer RegTech players targeting this space. The mid-market gap is real but competition exists.

## B2: Gap exists — 6/10
The "AI-generated impact assessment tailored to your specific license/products" is somewhat novel — most tools provide alerts but require human interpretation. However, Cube, Ascent (now Corlytics), and others are building exactly this capability. The gap is narrowing.

## C1: Technology can deliver — 7/10
Regulatory text classification and impact assessment is a good LLM use case. Karel can build this. The challenge is coverage breadth — monitoring 50+ regulatory bodies across jurisdictions requires continuous maintenance. Accuracy of impact assessment is critical (false negatives = missed compliance). Feasible but operationally demanding.

## C2: Wedge works — 8/10
Automated regulatory change alerting with AI impact summary is a clear, standalone wedge. Delivers immediate value (replace manual scanning of regulatory websites). High frequency (daily/weekly). Can be sold and used without deep integration. Excellent wedge.

## D1: Market reachable — 7/10
Dutch fintechs and payment providers: 200+ entities. European financial institutions broadly: thousands. Holland FinTech community, fintech conferences, compliance officer LinkedIn networks. The market is identifiable and reachable. Good TAM.

## D2: Unit economics — 7/10
At EUR 399-1,499/mo, replaces a significant portion of a compliance officer's time (EUR 60-80K/year salary). If it saves 20% of one compliance officer's time, the tool pays for itself. Clear ROI. Willingness-to-pay is supported by existing market (firms already pay Thomson Reuters).

## E1: Team advantage — 7/10
Jeroen's fintech background means he understands what financial firms need. Karel builds the AI pipeline. They know the Dutch fintech ecosystem. But neither has deep regulatory affairs experience — they'd need domain expertise (hire or partner). Good but not exceptional fit.

## E2: Timing catalyst — 6/10
The regulatory supercycle is real but has been building for years. No single sharp catalyst. "More regulations" is a trend, not an event. MiCA and DORA have specific deadlines (2025-2026) that create urgency for their specific domains but not for a horizontal reg-change tool. Moderate timing.
