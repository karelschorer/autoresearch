# CrewAI Ops — AI-Powered Airline Crew & Operations Management

## Problem Statement
Airlines lose EUR 50-200M/year to irregular operations (IROPS): delays, cancellations, crew duty-time violations, and passenger rebooking. Crew scheduling alone is a massive optimization problem — airlines have thousands of crew members with complex regulatory constraints (flight time limitations, rest requirements, qualification matrices). When disruptions hit, operations control centers make thousands of manual decisions under time pressure. Current tools (Jeppesen, AIMS) are legacy systems built in the 90s that handle scheduling but not real-time disruption management with AI optimization.

## Target Customer (ICP)
European airlines (LCCs and legacy carriers) with 50+ aircraft. Primary beachhead: mid-size European carriers (Transavia, Vueling, Norwegian, Wizz Air). These airlines are large enough to have significant IROPS costs but small enough that they don't have custom in-house AI teams.

## Proposed Solution & Wedge
AI-powered disruption management: real-time crew re-optimization during IROPS, automated passenger rebooking with fare-aware logic, and proactive delay prediction. Wedge: crew duty-time violation prevention — AI monitors crew schedules in real-time and flags/resolves violations before they cause cancellations.

## Pricing Model
- SaaS: EUR 20-50K/month based on fleet size
- Implementation: EUR 100-200K (one-time, includes system integration)
- Performance-based component: share of IROPS cost savings

## Go-to-Market
Karel's AF-KLM network for design partnership / reference customer. Aviation conferences (IATA Ops, Aviation Festival). Direct outreach to COO/VP Ops at European carriers. Airline consulting firms as channel partners.

## Defensibility & Moat
Karel's inside knowledge of airline ops processes and systems (Amadeus, AIMS, Altea). Integration with airline reservation/crew systems is deeply complex (12+ month implementation for competitors). Each airline's crew rules and operational patterns create unique training data. Aviation is a relationship-driven industry — early reference customers are powerful.
