# VaultOps — Crypto Treasury Management for Institutions

## Problem Statement
Crypto-native companies (exchanges, protocols, DAOs, funds) hold significant treasury in digital assets across multiple chains, wallets, and venues. Managing this treasury is operationally painful: tracking positions across 10+ wallets/chains, executing rebalances, managing staking yields, generating tax/accounting reports, and maintaining internal controls. Most teams use spreadsheets + block explorers + manual reconciliation. Enterprise treasury tools (Kyriba, GTreasury) don't support crypto. Crypto-native tools (Fireblocks, Anchorage) focus on custody, not treasury operations.

## Target Customer (ICP)
Crypto-native companies with EUR 5M+ in digital asset treasury: exchanges, lending platforms, DeFi protocols with treasury, crypto funds, and corporate treasuries holding crypto. Beachhead: European crypto firms (MiCA-compliant entities that now need proper treasury governance).

## Proposed Solution & Wedge
Unified treasury dashboard that aggregates positions across chains/wallets/venues, provides real-time P&L, automates rebalancing workflows, generates accounting-ready reports (IFRS/Dutch GAAP), and enforces treasury policies (exposure limits, counterparty risk). Wedge: automated multi-chain portfolio tracking + IFRS-compliant crypto accounting reports.

## Pricing Model
- Starter (EUR 499/mo): Up to 20 wallets, 5 chains, basic reporting
- Professional (EUR 1,499/mo): Unlimited wallets/chains, rebalancing workflows, tax reports
- Enterprise (EUR 5-10K/mo): Custom policies, API integrations, dedicated support, audit trail

## Go-to-Market
Jeroen's Kraken/crypto network. Amsterdam crypto ecosystem (DACT, Dutch Blockchain Coalition). Direct outreach to CFOs/treasury at MiCA-licensed entities. Crypto fund administrator partnerships.

## Defensibility & Moat
Jeroen built and sold a crypto data product — knows the data infra pain firsthand. Multi-chain integration complexity creates a technical moat (each chain/protocol requires custom integration). Accounting logic per jurisdiction is hard to replicate. Treasury policy engine becomes system of record (switching costs).
