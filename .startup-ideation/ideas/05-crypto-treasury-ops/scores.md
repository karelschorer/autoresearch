# VaultOps — Scores (Cycle 0 Baseline)

**Total: 68/100**

## A1: Problem is real — 7/10
Crypto treasuries are genuinely painful to manage — multi-chain, multi-wallet, manual reconciliation. Anyone who has worked in crypto operations knows this pain. However, the number of entities with EUR 5M+ crypto treasury is limited. The pain is real for those who have it, but the population is small.

## A2: Problem is growing — 7/10
MiCA requires proper treasury governance for licensed entities. Institutional crypto adoption is growing (slowly post-bear-market). More DeFi protocols are professionalizing treasury management. Moderate growth, dependent on crypto market recovery.

## B1: Alternatives inadequate — 7/10
Fireblocks and Anchorage focus on custody, not treasury ops. Enterprise treasury tools (Kyriba) don't support crypto. Crypto accounting tools (Cryptio, Bitwave) focus on tax, not operations. The treasury operations layer is genuinely underserved. Some overlap with portfolio trackers but none offer the full ops workflow.

## B2: Gap exists — 7/10
The specific gap (multi-chain aggregation + IFRS accounting + rebalancing workflows + treasury policy enforcement) is not addressed by any single tool. Crypto firms cobble together 3-5 tools + spreadsheets. The gap is clear and specific.

## C1: Technology can deliver — 7/10
Multi-chain data aggregation is technically solvable but operationally complex (each chain/protocol has different APIs, data formats, update frequencies). Jeroen has built crypto data infrastructure. IFRS crypto accounting rules are complex but finite. The tech challenge is integration breadth, not AI difficulty.

## C2: Wedge works — 7/10
Multi-chain portfolio tracking + accounting reports is a viable wedge — delivers immediate value without requiring full treasury policy adoption. Solves the #1 pain point (knowing what you actually hold across all wallets/chains). Good standalone value.

## D1: Market reachable — 5/10
European crypto firms with significant treasuries: maybe 50-150 entities. Very niche. Reachable through Jeroen's network and crypto communities, but the TAM is small. International expansion (US, Asia) increases TAM but adds regulatory complexity.

## E1: Team advantage — 8/10
Jeroen built and sold a crypto data product to Kraken. He understands crypto data infrastructure, exchange operations, and what crypto firms need. Karel's AI/ML adds analytics capabilities. This team has genuine domain credibility in crypto operations. Strong fit.

## D2: Unit economics — 5/10
At EUR 499-1,499/mo, competes with manual spreadsheet work (free) and basic portfolio trackers (free/cheap). Crypto firms are notoriously cost-sensitive. Enterprise tier (EUR 5-10K/mo) is justified for large treasuries but the beachhead customers are smaller. Willingness-to-pay is uncertain.

## E2: Timing catalyst — 8/10
MiCA treasury governance requirements are new and mandatory. Crypto market recovery creates more treasury to manage. Institutional adoption brings professional treasury expectations. Multiple converging catalysts, though none as sharp as a single deadline.
