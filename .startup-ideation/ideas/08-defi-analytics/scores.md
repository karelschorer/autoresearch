# ChainPulse — Scores (Cycle 0 Baseline)

**Total: 65/100**

## A1: Problem is real — 7/10
DeFi exploits have caused EUR 3B+ in losses. Institutional investors need risk assessment before deploying capital. The pain is real for institutions entering DeFi. But many institutions simply avoid DeFi entirely rather than seeking risk tools — the TAM may be more "DeFi-native funds" than "institutions entering DeFi."

## A2: Problem is growing — 7/10
Institutional DeFi adoption is growing (slowly). New protocols launch weekly, increasing the risk surface. MiCA may bring more regulated entities into DeFi adjacent activities. But DeFi growth depends heavily on crypto market cycles — bearish markets = less DeFi activity = less demand.

## B1: Alternatives inadequate — 6/10
Chainalysis, Nansen, Arkham Intelligence, and DefiLlama offer various analytics. Gauntlet and Chaos Labs do protocol risk assessment. The space has multiple well-funded players. However, none offer a simple "institutional risk score" product — most are complex analytics platforms requiring expertise to interpret.

## B2: Gap exists — 7/10
The "simple risk score API for institutional compliance" gap is real. Most DeFi analytics require crypto-native expertise to interpret. An abstracted risk rating that non-crypto-native compliance teams can use for allocation decisions is underserved. The gap is in simplification and institutional packaging, not in raw data.

## C1: Technology can deliver — 7/10
Multi-chain data indexing is technically complex but proven (The Graph, Dune, Flipside). Risk modeling requires combining on-chain data with off-chain signals. Jeroen understands crypto data infrastructure. Karel can build the ML pipeline. Technically feasible, not trivial.

## C2: Wedge works — 8/10
Protocol risk score API is an excellent wedge — simple to build, simple to sell, delivers immediate value. Funds can integrate a single API call into their allocation process. No heavy platform adoption needed. Clean, narrow wedge.

## D1: Market reachable — 5/10
Institutional DeFi participants in Europe: maybe 50-200 entities (crypto funds, family offices, protocol treasuries). Small TAM. Reachable through crypto fund networks and DeFi communities. International (US, Asia) expands TAM but adds competition.

## D2: Unit economics — 6/10
At EUR 499-1,999/mo, competes with free on-chain data and internal risk teams. The value is risk avoidance — hard to quantify until a loss happens. "Insurance against DeFi exploits" is a tough sell to funds that pride themselves on their own research. Moderate willingness-to-pay.

## E1: Team advantage — 7/10
Jeroen's crypto data product experience (Masterpiece) and Kraken connections are directly relevant. He understands on-chain data infrastructure. Karel's ML skills add the analytical layer. Good fit, though Jeroen's experience is more centralized crypto than DeFi specifically.

## E2: Timing catalyst — 5/10
No sharp catalyst. Institutional DeFi adoption is gradual. MiCA doesn't directly require DeFi risk scoring. The opportunity has existed since 2021 (post-DeFi Summer). No urgency beyond "crypto market recovery" which is cyclical. Weak timing.
