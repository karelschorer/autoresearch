# DocMine — AI-Powered Financial Document Intelligence

## Problem Statement
Investment analysts, auditors, and corporate finance teams spend 40-60% of their time extracting and comparing data from financial documents: earnings reports, annual filings, loan agreements, prospectuses. The documents are semi-structured (PDFs, XBRL), inconsistent across companies, and require domain expertise to interpret. Current solutions are either manual (Excel + reading) or enterprise platforms (Bloomberg Terminal, S&P Capital IQ) costing EUR 20-50K/year per seat.

## Target Customer (ICP)
Mid-size investment firms, PE/VC funds, corporate finance teams, and audit firms in Europe with 5-50 analysts. Too small for Bloomberg Terminal seats for everyone, too sophisticated for manual Excel work. Beachhead: Dutch/European PE and VC funds.

## Proposed Solution & Wedge
AI pipeline that ingests financial documents (PDFs, XBRL, earnings transcripts), extracts structured data (revenue, margins, covenants, risk factors), and enables natural-language querying across document sets. Wedge: automated earnings call analysis — upload a transcript, get structured financial summary with sentiment analysis and guidance extraction within minutes.

## Pricing Model
- Starter (EUR 299/mo): 50 documents/month, basic extraction, natural language search
- Professional (EUR 999/mo): 500 documents, cross-document comparison, custom extraction templates
- Enterprise (EUR 3-5K/mo): Unlimited, API access, custom integrations, white-label

## Go-to-Market
Amsterdam financial community (Zuidas). PE/VC network. Finance-focused LinkedIn content. Holland FinTech membership. Partnership with audit/advisory firms for white-label.

## Defensibility & Moat
Extraction templates and financial ontology improve with each customer's documents (data flywheel). Jeroen's fintech background provides credibility with financial buyers. Custom extraction templates per client create switching costs. Enterprise integration depth locks in accounts.
