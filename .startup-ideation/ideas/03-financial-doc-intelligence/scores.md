# DocMine — Scores (Cycle 0 Baseline)

**Total: 60/100**

## A1: Problem is real — 7/10
Financial analysts do spend enormous time on document review and data extraction. McKinsey estimates 40-60% of analyst time goes to data gathering. The pain is documented. However, the highest-value analysts are at firms that already pay for Bloomberg/Capital IQ, and junior analysts doing manual work are cheap labor (EUR 40-60K salary). The ROI case depends on replacing cheap labor with expensive software.

## A2: Problem is growing — 6/10
The volume of financial documents is growing slowly (more filings, more complexity), but this isn't a step-function increase. ESG reporting (CSRD) adds new document types. Moderate growth, no explosive catalyst.

## B1: Alternatives inadequate — 4/10
This space is extremely crowded. AlphaSense, Sentieo (now AlphaSense), Kensho (S&P), Visible Alpha, and many others offer AI-powered financial document analysis. Bloomberg is adding AI features. ChatGPT/Claude can already parse financial documents well. The mid-market gap may exist for firms that can't afford AlphaSense (EUR 30K+/seat), but competition is intense.

## B2: Gap exists — 5/10
The "mid-market financial document intelligence" niche is real but small. Most PE/VC funds either use AlphaSense/PitchBook or have analysts who use ChatGPT directly. The value-add of a purpose-built tool over a general LLM is unclear for many use cases.

## C1: Technology can deliver — 7/10
LLMs are good at financial document parsing. Karel can build this. But accuracy for financial data extraction needs to be very high (99%+), and hallucination risk on financial numbers is a real concern. The technology works but the accuracy bar is demanding.

## C2: Wedge works — 6/10
Earnings call analysis is a reasonable wedge but there are already many tools doing this (Daloopa, FinChat, etc.). The wedge isn't differentiated enough. Would need a more specific entry point.

## D1: Market reachable — 6/10
European PE/VC funds are identifiable (a few hundred) but hard to reach — they're inundated with software pitches. The Zuidas network could help but neither founder has deep PE/VC connections. Moderate reachability.

## D2: Unit economics — 6/10
At EUR 299-999/mo, competes with analyst time (cheap) and free LLM usage. The value prop needs to be 10x better than "paste into ChatGPT" to justify a subscription. Hard to quantify value beyond time savings. ROI is moderate.

## E1: Team advantage — 5/10
Jeroen has fintech background but Masterpiece was an NFT data product, not financial document analysis. Karel's AI skills are relevant but generic for this space. Neither founder has deep PE/VC or investment analyst experience. Moderate fit, no unique edge.

## E2: Timing catalyst — 8/10
CSRD (EU sustainability reporting) creates massive new document volume starting 2024-2026. This is a genuine catalyst — thousands of European companies must produce detailed sustainability reports for the first time, creating new analysis demand. However, the competition is already responding to this catalyst.
