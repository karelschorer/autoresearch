# PriceAgent — Scores (Cycle 0 Baseline)

**Total: 59/100**

## A1: Problem is real — 7/10
E-commerce pricing is a real pain for mid-market sellers. Managing thousands of SKUs across marketplaces manually is time-consuming. Buy-box competition on Amazon/Bol.com directly impacts revenue. The problem is documented and quantifiable (wrong pricing = lost sales or margin erosion).

## A2: Problem is growing — 6/10
E-commerce continues to grow. More sellers, more SKUs, more marketplaces. But the growth is steady, not explosive. Dynamic pricing has been a trend for 10+ years. No step-function change expected.

## B1: Alternatives inadequate — 4/10
Repricer tools are a mature market. Feedvisor, Seller Snap, BQool, and dozens of others offer AI-powered repricing for Amazon. Competera, Intelligence Node, and Prisync cover broader e-commerce. The space is crowded with established, well-funded competitors. Bol.com-specific is underserved but that's a tiny market.

## B2: Gap exists — 4/10
The "intelligent pricing that considers demand elasticity + inventory + margins" is what every competitor claims to do. The Bol.com-specific angle is a gap but Bol.com is primarily a Dutch/Belgian marketplace — very narrow. The gap is more geographic niche than functional capability.

## C1: Technology can deliver — 7/10
Pricing optimization is well-understood ML (demand forecasting, elasticity estimation, optimization). Karel's ML background is directly relevant. The technology works. The challenge is data quality and marketplace API limitations, not algorithm difficulty.

## C2: Wedge works — 7/10
Bol.com buy-box optimization is a clear, narrow wedge with immediate ROI. Sellers can measure the impact in days (sales volume, margin). Good standalone value. Quick time-to-value.

## D1: Market reachable — 7/10
Dutch marketplace sellers: thousands of sellers on Bol.com and Amazon.nl. Reachable through seller communities, Bol.com partner ecosystem, e-commerce events. Good TAM. But most are small sellers (EUR <500K revenue) with low willingness-to-pay.

## D2: Unit economics — 5/10
At EUR 149-499/mo, competes with cheaper repricing tools (EUR 50-200/mo). Value is clear (more sales/margin) but the market is price-sensitive. Mid-market sellers (EUR 1-20M) are the sweet spot but they have many options. Race to the bottom on pricing in a competitive market.

## E1: Team advantage — 4/10
Karel's ML skills are relevant but pricing optimization is not a novel application of ML. Jeroen's fintech/crypto background is irrelevant to e-commerce pricing. Neither founder has e-commerce or marketplace experience. No domain advantage, no network into the buyer persona. Poor team fit.

## E2: Timing catalyst — 3/10
No catalyst. E-commerce pricing optimization has been a thing for 10+ years. No regulatory change, no market shift, no technology breakthrough creating urgency. This is a mature market with established players. Weak timing.
